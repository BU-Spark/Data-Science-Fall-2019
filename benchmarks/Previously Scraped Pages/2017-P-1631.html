    02/21/2018   RE#4: It is noted that this appeal entered when it was transferred from the docket of 17-J-578, a single justice matter in which only Bruce Boguslav filed an affidavit of indigency. Accordingly, only his appeal is before this court. To the extent Linda Boguslav seeks to appeal, she is to pay the requisite filing fee or file a motion to waive the fee and an affidavit of indigency, due on or before 2/28/18. It is noted that appellant sought a stay of proceedings for purposes of obtaining transcripts from the Northeast Housing Court proceedings. Appellant seeks a further stay so as to prosecute a recently-filed Rule 60(b) motion, and to prosecute an appeal from the trial court's order on the payment of use and occupancy pending appeal. Since the appeal has been entered, leave from the court is required prior to the appellant's filing and the trial court's consideration of a Rule 60(b) motion. To date such leave has neither been granted nor requested, so a stay is not appropriate for that purpose. As the transcripts have not yet been produced, and in order that the defendant may prosecute his appeal from the use and occupancy order, appellate proceedings STAYED to 03/20/2018. Status report due 03/20/2018 concerning the production of the transcripts and the appeal from the use and occupancy order. *Notice/attest/Kerman, J. *Notice.

    03/05/2018   RE#6: Appellant's request for leave to file a Rule 60(b) motion in the Housing Court is denied. To the extent the Appellants seek a stay of appellate proceedings in this summary process appeal, in order to pursue in the Housing Court a motion for Rule 60(b) relief, the motion is denied. However, this appeal remains stayed pursuant to this Court's order of 2/21/18 while the Appellants obtain transcripts of the Housing Court proceedings relevant to this appeal in both the Worcester and Northeast Housing Courts. In addition, notwithstanding the fact that this appeal has been pending in this court since 12/28/17, and, in contravention of an order issued on 11/6/17 by Associate Justice Kerman of the Housing Court, staying all use and occupancy payments, it appears that a different justice of the Housing Court issued an order on 2/6/18, reinstating use and occupancy payments. That 2/6/18 order issued without leave of the Appeals Court or a single justice thereof; moreover, Appellants have filed a notice of appeal from that order as well. It therefore appears that the pending appeal should also be stayed pending the assembly and transmission of the Appellants' request to the single justice for review pursuant to G.L. c. 239, s. 5 of the 2/6/18 bond order. Appellate proceedings remain stayed until 3/20/18; a status report is due at that time, addressing the readiness of any outstanding transcripts and the assembly and transmission of Appellants' request pursuant to G.L. c. 239, s. 5 for review of the 2/6/18 order for periodic payments. (Hanlon, J.) . *Notice/Attest/Muirhead, J./Kerman, J.
    03/05/2018   RE#9: In the normal course, a motion to dismiss a summary process appeal for failure to make periodic payments should be addressed to the "court rendering the judgment," here the Housing Court. See G.L. c. 239, s. 5(h). Nonetheless, as it appears that the bond order is still subject to single justice review under G.L. c. 239, s. 5(f) and (g), the motion is denied. (Hanlon, J.) *Notice/Attest/Muirhead, J./Kerman, J.
