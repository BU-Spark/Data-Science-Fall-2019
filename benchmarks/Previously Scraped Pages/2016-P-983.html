    12/22/2017 #23 ORDER: (RE#21) Defendants L&W and Levy have requested, and this panel allowed, an award of appellate attorney's fees and costs after we affirmed a judge's finding that counsel for Upper Crust was not in contempt of a previously issued injunction In accordance with our panel decision, the defendants filed an application for attorney's fees pursuant to the protocol set forth in Fabre v. Walton, 441 Mass. 9 (2004). They seek $36,149.50 plus interest, of which $33,006.50 are attorney's fees and $1,571.50 are costs, which they request be doubled. See G. L. c. 211A, § 15; Mass. R.A.P. 25, as appearing in 376 Mass. 949 (1979). A determination of the amount of reasonable appellate attorney's fees is "largely discretionary." Stowe v. Bologna, 417 Mass. 199, 203 (1994)(quotation omitted). In making such a determination, we "properly exercise[] independent judgment concerning the request's reasonableness." Id. at 204. In determining whether a fee is reasonable, we do not focus on the amounts billed or the amount in controversy, but rather on "several factors, including 'the nature of the case and the issues presented, the time and labor required, . . . the result obtained, the experience, reputation and ability of the attorney, the usual price charged for similar services by other attorneys in the same area, and the amount of awards in similar cases.'" Berman v. Linnane, 434 Mass. 301, 303 (2001), quoting from Linthicum v. Archambault, 379 Mass. 381, 388-389 (1979). We are not obliged to "review and allow or disallow each individual item in the bill, but [may] consider the bill as a whole." Ibid. The plaintiff does not contest the hourly rates submitted by the two attorneys that handled the appeal for the defendants. Rather, the plaintiff's opposition centers on the 50.1 hours spent by the associate attorney representing the defendants to research and draft the appellate brief and requests that time be reduced to no more than twenty-five hours. Further, the plaintiff contends there are duplicative and excessive charges for simple administrative tasks, and asserts that the amount of time billed for preparing for and attending oral argument was excessive.[1] Finally, the plaintiff contests the $1,524 in legal research charges included in the defendants' costs as not recoverable and lacking sufficient specificity in the petition. Having considered the defendants' petition and affidavits, the plaintiff's opposition, the appellate briefs and record, and the appropriate factors detailed above, we award $23,644 in appellate attorney's fees and $1,571.50 in costs, which we do not double. Accordingly, we therefore award the defendants appellate attorney's fees and costs of appeal in the amount of $25,215.50, with no interest awarded. Any proceeding to enforce this order shall be commenced in the Superior Court. (Henry, Desmond & Shin, JJ.[2]). Notice/attest/MacLeod-Mancuso, J.
