    12/28/2010 #5 ANNOUNCEMENT: The Justices are soliciting amicus briefs. 1. Whether the holding in Commonwealth v. Kebreau, 454 Mass. 287, 292-296 (2009), which permits testimony from two first complaint witnesses in certain limited circumstances, applies where, as allegedly happened here, a complainant is subjected during a given time period to two separate and distinct types of sexual offenses, e.g., indecent touchings and rape; initially reveals to a first complaint witness only the lesser of the offenses; and then, years later, reveals to a second witness the more serious of the offenses. 2. Whether the first complaint doctrine set forth in Commonwealth v. King, 445 Mass. 217 (2005), should be abolished or amended. 3. If the doctrine is to be abolished or amended, what other rules should be put in place, or in what way should the existing doctrine be rewritten, to adequately protect and balance the competing interests of the Commonwealth and defendants in sexual assault cases. The case is tentatively scheduled for March, 2011. Amicus submissions are due two weeks before the first day of the sitting in which the case is scheduled..
