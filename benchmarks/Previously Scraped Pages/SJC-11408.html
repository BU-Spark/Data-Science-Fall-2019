    04/02/2013 #3 ANNOUNCEMENT: The Justices are soliciting amicus briefs. These three cases present issues with respect to the nature and extent of the authority of Special Magistrates and Superior Court judges appointed pursuant to an Order of Assignment by the Chief Justice of the Superior Court, see Mass. R. Crim. P. 47, "to preside over criminal proceedings in connection with cases relating to the William A. Hinton State Laboratory Institute in Jamaica Plain." Specifically the issues raised are: whether a Special Magistrate may vacate a guilty plea and conduct a partial guilty plea colloquy; whether a plea conducted under the procedure established under the Order of Appointment is valid; whether a Special Magistrate (who originally allowed the defendant's motion to stay execution of his sentence) or a Superior Court judge (who considered the issue anew and also allowed the motion) had the authority to consider and allow the motion before a new trial motion had been allowed or even filed, and no appeal was pending.

    05/17/2013 #17 ORDER: The single justice reserved and reported the following question to the court: "Does a special magistrate appointed by the Chief Justice of the Superior Court pursuant to Mass. R. Crim. P. 47, or a judge of the Superior Court, have the authority to allow a defendant's motion to stay execution of his sentence, then being served, pending disposition of the defendant's motion for new trial?" The answer to the question is that a trial judge has the inherent power to stay sentences "for the exceptional reasons permitted by law." Commonwealth v. McLaughlin, 431 Mass. 506, 520 (2000). In the circumstances arising from the allegations of misconduct at the William H. Hinton State Laboratory Institute, a judge in the Superior Court does have the authority to allow a defendant's motion to stay the execution of his sentence, then being served, pending disposition of the defendant's motion for a new trial. A special magistrate does not have the authority to allow such a motion, but may make proposed findings of fact and rulings of law on the motion pursuant to Mass. R. Crim. P. 47. The opinion of this court will follow. The single justice is directed to vacate the order she issued on February 15, 2013, that stayed the order of a judge in the Superior Court dated February 13, 2013. The Superior Court judge had stayed the execution of the defendant's sentence pending the disposition of his motion for a new trial. By the Court. Notice.
