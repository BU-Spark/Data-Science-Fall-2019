    05/02/2013 #2 ANNOUNCEMENT: The Justices are soliciting amicus briefs. Whether certain forest land owned by the plaintiff, a land conservation organization, qualifies for charitable tax exemption pursuant to G. L. c. 59, § 5, Clause Third; whether the Appellate Tax Board erred in ruling, among other things, that the plaintiff's purposes and activities do not fit into the established realm of traditional charities according to Massachusetts case law, that the property was not sufficiently open to and accessible by the public to qualify for tax exemption, and that the plaintiff failed to demonstrate a sufficiently active appropriation of the subject property to achieve a public benefit.
