    02/15/2017 #21 ORDER: At present, this case is impounded, pursuant to the Superior Court judge's order dated March 4, 2013, which allowed, without stating any additional grounds, the parties' "motion for impoundment of the docket by assent and in camera review" (S.A.44-48), thus requiring the use of pseudonyms for all parties in this appeal. See S.J.C. Rule 1:15, § 2. The appropriateness of using a pseudonym for the plaintiff is clear. This applies, as well, to the two victims. However, it is far from clear why pseudonyms are appropriate for any other persons or entities, including the defendants. By close of business, February 23, 2017, the parties shall notify this court whether they oppose lifting the impoundment of the case itself, and, if so, on what basis. See Adams v. Adams, 459 Mass. 361, 362 n.1 (2011) (recognizing the authority of an appellate court to modify extant impoundment orders issued by the trial court). See generally Republican Co. v. Appeals Court, 442 Mass. 218, 223 (2004) ("The exercise of the power to restrict access. . . to judicial records is to be 'strictly construed in favor of the general principle of publicity'"), quoting from Commonwealth v. Blondin, 324 Mass. 564, 571 (1949), cert. denied, 339 U.S. (1950). To the extent that the parties do not oppose using the real names of people or entities (other the plaintiff and the victims), but nevertheless have concerns that removing the current impoundment order could reveal the identity of the plaintiff or victims, they shall propose specific measures (e.g., redactions from, of limited impoundment of, documents in the record) to address such concerns (Wolohojian, Milkey & Shin, JJ.). Notice
    02/22/2017 #22 Joint Response to paper #21, filed by John Doe, American Guaranty and Liability Co, George Rockas, H. Ernest Stone.
    02/27/2017 #23 ORDER: This case was impounded pursuant to the Superior Court judge's order dated March 4, 2013, which allowed, without stating any additional grounds, the parties' "motion for impoundment of the docket by assent and in camera review." By order dated February 15, 2017, this court sua sponte requested that the parties address the impoundment status of the case. Based on the joint response submitted by the parties, we vacate the existing impoundment order to the extent it applies to the appellate case itself, this court's docket, and the parties' appellate briefs. The order is otherwise retained, and we specifically note that the record appendix, and the two supplemental appendices shall remain impounded. (Wolohojian, Milkey & Shin, JJ.). *Notice/Attest.
