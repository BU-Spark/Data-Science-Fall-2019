    10/18/2017   RE#11: The defendant is now serving a two-year committed sentence, which was originally suspended, but which was imposed by the trial judge as a result of a violation of his conditions of probation. The defendant seeks to stay further execution of that sentence. He has filed an application for stay in the Dorchester Division of the Boston Municipal Court, but it has not as yet been ruled upon.The Commonwealth argues that three of the violations found by the judge are supported, but concedes in its brief that there was no support for one of the alleged violations upon which the revocation of probation was premised, and that the case must be remanded for resentencing in light of that. Given this, I find it likely that, upon consideration of the merits of the appeal, a panel of this court will remand the case for such resentencing. Requests for stay should ordinarily be considered in the first instance by the trial court, see Mass. R. App. P. 6(b)(1). Because Judge Tynes is both the trial judge and the judge who presided over the probation surrender hearing, he is in the best position to determine the likely consequence on sentencing if any of the error identified by the Commonwealth. I therefore am ordering the motion now pending in the Dorchester Division of the Boston Municipal Court for stay of further execution of sentence be assigned to Judge Tynes, and be ruled upon by him within ten days. He may, of course, request further briefing from the parties. The motion for stay presented to me as single justice is denied without prejudice to refiling following the decision on the motion for stay now pending in the Dorchester Division of the Boston Municipal Court. (Rubin, J.). *Notice/Attest/Tyne.
