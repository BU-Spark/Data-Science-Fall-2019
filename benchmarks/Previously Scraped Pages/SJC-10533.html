    06/16/2010 #37 ORDER: This court granted Mark Reznik certain relief on his petition pursuant to G. L. c. 211, sec. 3. Reznik v. District Court Dep't. of the Trial Court, 456 Mass. 1001 (2010). Reznik has filed a motion for costs and a request (in a paper titled "Request to Put the End to the Endless Discrimination") that the case be recaptioned to identify the District Court judge by name. Both the motion for costs and the request for recaptioning are denied. As to the motion for costs, the only parties properly named as respondents to Reznik's G. L. c. 211, sec. 3, petition, served with process, and made parties to the case were the District Court and a judge thereof. See Mass. R. Civ. P. 10 (a) ("In the complaint the title of the action shall include the names of all the parties...."). Costs cannot be assessed against the Commonwealth or agencies or officers thereof without "specific affirmative authority." e.g., Ware v. Commonwealth, 409 Mass. 89, 90 (1991), quoting Broadhurst v. Director of the Div. of Employment Sec., 373 Mass. 720, 722 (1977). See Mass. R. A. P. 26 (b); Mass. R. Civ. P. 54 (d). Reznik identifies no such authority, and we are aware of none. Reznik has also requested that costs be assessed against Bay Avary Associates, Inc. (Bay Avary), his opponent in the underlying District Court proceedings. However, Bay Avary was never made a party to the proceedings in either the county court or the full court. As to Reznik's request that the rescript opinion be recaptioned, our longstanding convention is to name only the court, and not the judge, where, as here, the petitioner complains solely about actions the judge took in his or her official capacity. Reznik has not shown either that this practice is improper or that he has been harmed in any way by it. Accordingly, the request is denied. By the Court. (Notice to parties.)
