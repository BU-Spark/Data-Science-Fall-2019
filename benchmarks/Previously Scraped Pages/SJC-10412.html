    04/02/2009 #4 ORDER (By the Court): The defendants appeal from a judgment of a single justice of this court granting a petition filed by the Commonwealth pursuant to GLc211, s3. The judgment vacated an interlocutory order by a judge in the District Court in each defendant's criminal case that would have required the Commonwealth to produce "source code" for a breathalyzer machine. The judgment further directed the judge to hold a hearing in accordance with Comm. v. Lampron, 441 Mass. 265 (2004), before determining whether the source code must be produced. The appeal is presently before us on a memorandum and record appendix filed by the defendants, which, it appears, they submitted in an attempt to comply with SJC Rule 2:21.... That rule applies only in cases where "a single justice denies relief from a challenged interlocutory ruling in the trial court,"...and not where a single justice grants relief from an interlocutory ruling. The rule does not apply here. The defendants are thus free to proceed with their appeal in the ordinary course. The timetable for briefing will commence on the date of this order. We express no view at this juncture whether the Commonwealth was entitled to extraordinary review and relief pursuant to GLc211, s3. The threshold question as to the appropriateness of the single justice's reviewing the matter under GLc211, s3, as well as the correctness of her granting relief on the merits of the petition, shall be addressed by the parties in their briefs. (Notice to counsel.)
