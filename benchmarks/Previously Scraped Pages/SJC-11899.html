    03/09/2016 #11 RESCRIPT (Full Opinion): The evidence at trial was sufficient to support the jury's verdict that the defendants retaliated against Esler because she exercised her right to twelve weeks of FMLA leave. We further conclude that the issue of front pay in an FMLA retaliation case should be reserved for the judge, and that the judge did not abuse her discretion or otherwise err in determining that the evidence at trial was insufficient to support an award of front pay. Accordingly, we reverse in part and affirm in part the entry of judgment for the defendants on the defendants' motion for judgment n.o.v. We remand the case to the Superior Court for further proceedings with respect to the defendants' alternative request for a new trial. (By the Court)

    07/22/2016 #20 ORDER: In Esler v. Sylvia-Reardon, 473 Mass. 775 (2016), we reversed the entry of judgment notwithstanding the verdict with respect to Marie Esler's claim that Massachusetts General Hospital terminated her employment in retaliation for her exercise of the right to take medical leave under the Federal Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA), 29 U.S.C. § 2601, et seq. (2012); affirmed the judge's order with respect to front pay; and remanded the case to the Superior Court for further proceedings concerning the defendants' alternative request for a new trial. A hearing was held in the Superior Court, but the proceedings in that court have not yet been concluded. The plaintiff has filed a petition for an award of attorneys' fees of approximately $258,701.25, and costs in the amount of $ 5,195.05, for work performed in the Superior Court through March 15, 2015 (Superior Court petition), as well as a motion to enlarge the time for filing and for other relief. She has also filed a separate motion seeking additional time to file a petition for appellate fees and to renew her petition previously filed in the Appeals Court (SJC petition). The Superior Court petition is denied without prejudice. That petition seeks an award of attorneys' fees and costs relating to proceedings in the Superior Court. The appropriate forum for such a request is the Superior Court. See T & D Video, Inc. v. City of Revere, 450 Mass. 107, 116 (2007). We express no opinion as to the timeliness or reasonableness of any such request that may be filed. Those issues, and any claims or defenses that might be raised, including issues of waiver, may be litigated in the Superior Court. We take no action on the plaintiff's requests to renew two petitions filed in the Appeals Court seeking attorneys' fees and costs. The Appeals Court is the appropriate forum to address whether the petitions previously filed in that court should be "renewed." The SJC petition also is denied without prejudice. The case has been remanded to the Superior Court for further proceedings concerning the defendants' alternative request for a new trial, and those proceedings have not yet been concluded. In the circumstances of this case, we cannot say with certainty that the plaintiff has emerged as the prevailing party on her FMLA claim. Having made a request for attorneys' fees and costs in the brief she filed in this court, the plaintiff may seek an award with respect to the proceedings before this court if and when she emerges as the prevailing party on her FMLA claim when the Superior Court proceedings have been concluded. See T&D Video, Inc., supra at 115-116. Any issues associated with such a petition, including questions of waiver, reasonableness and timeliness, may be raised at that time. By the Court.

    03/07/2017 #28 ORDER: The plaintiff's request for costs and attorney's fees incurred in the proceedings before this court is hereby allowed, although not in the full amount requested. Having carefully reviewed the plaintiff's motion, her supporting material, the defendant's opposition, the application and opposition to further appellate review, and the briefs and other materials filed here and in the Appeals Court, however, we conclude that a reduction in the amount of fees is appropriate. See Stowe v. Bologna, 417 Mass. 199, 203-204 (1994); Linthicum v. Archambault, 379 Mass. 381, 388 (1979) (considering 'nature of the case and the issues presented, the time and labor required, the amount of the damages involved, the result obtained, the experience, reputation and ability of the attorney, the usual price charged for similar services by other attorneys in the same area, and the amount of awards in similar cases'). The case previously had been briefed and argued in the Appeals Court, the law was not complex given the experience of the attorneys, and the factual record was not extensive. Reducing the fees also ensures that our award is not disproportionate to what the Appeals Court panel determined to be appropriate for the brief and argument in the main appeal. The defendant will therefore receive an award of fees in the amount of $21,000 and costs in the amount of $591.13 for the proceedings in this court. This order shall be enforceable by the trial court, if necessary. (By the Court)
