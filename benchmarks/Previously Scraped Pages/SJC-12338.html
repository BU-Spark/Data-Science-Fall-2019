    11/03/2017 #6 ORDER: The defendant has been charged in the Boston Municipal Court with larceny from a person, assault and battery, and assault and battery with a dangerous weapon. Just before trial was set to commence the Commonwealth filed a motion seeking the production by the defendant of a certain surveillance video.[1] A trial court judge denied the motion, and the Commonwealth sought relief from a single justice of this court pursuant to G. L. c. 211, § 3.[2] In its petition the Commonwealth argued that the video is "crucial evidence" without which it cannot obtain a fair trial. The single justice denied the petition. The case is now before this court pursuant to S.J.C. Rule 2:21, as amended, 434 Mass. 1301 (2001). Because the underlying trial court order is interlocutory, the rule requires the petitioner to "set forth the reasons why review of the trial court decision cannot adequately be obtained on appeal from any final adverse judgment in the trial court or by other available means." Rule 2:21 (2). That is the single, very limited focus of the rule, and on that point we agree with the Commonwealth that it cannot obtain appellate review of the judge's order by any other means. The appeal therefore satisfies the Rule 2:21 standard and may now proceed, with briefing and oral argument. The Commonwealth should be mindful, however, as it pursues this appeal, that the fact that it has no other remedy does not automatically entitle it to consideration of the substantive merits of its claim. See Commonwealth v. Richardson, 454 Mass. 1005, 1005-1006 (2009); Commonwealth v. Narea, 454 Mass. 1003, 1004 n. 1 (2009); Commonwealth v. Cook, 380 Mass. 314, 319 (1980). It will first be incumbent on the Commonwealth to demonstrate that this is the type of "rare case" and "exceptional circumstance" that requires the exercise of the court's extraordinary power of general superintendence; more specifically, that the single justice abused her discretion in declining to employ the court's extraordinary superintendence power in these circumstances. See Commonwealth v. Barros, 460 Mass. 1015, 1016 (2011); Commonwealth v. Richardson, supra. Where this is an appeal from the single justice's decision, and not a de novo review of the petition or second bite at the apple, it will not be enough for the Commonwealth simply to repeat the same arguments to this court that it pressed unsuccessfully before the single justice. Commonwealth v. Samuels, 456 Mass. 1025, 1027 n. 1 (2010). See Commonwealth v. Barros, supra at 1017. The full court will reach the substantive merits only if it first determines that the case presents the type of extraordinary situation requiring consideration of the merits under G. L. c. 211, § 3, and that the single justice abused her discretion in ruling otherwise. By the Court. [1] It appears that the Commonwealth had previously provided the video to the defendant pursuant to Mass. R. Crim. P. 14, as part of pretrial discovery, but either failed to retain a copy for itself or was unable to locate its copy. [2] The trial court proceedings have been stayed pending the disposition of the G. L. c. 211, § 3, petition.
