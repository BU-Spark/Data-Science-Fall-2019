    10/12/2010 #4 ORDER (By the Court): ORDER. Bar counsel filed a three-count petition for discipline with the Board of Bar Overseers, alleging that the respondent attorney commingled and intentionally converted funds paid in advance as a retainer, and that she engaged in other misconduct. A hearing committee of the board found that the respondent intentionally misused the retainer funds, and that her client was actually deprived of the funds at the time he was entitled to them. Taking into account factors it found in mitigation, the committee recommended the respondent be suspended from the practice of law for one year, with six months of the suspension suspended for two years on probationary terms. (R.A. 148). Bar counsel appealed to the board from the recommended sanction, arguing that the presumptive sanctions described in Matter of the Discipline of an Attorney, 392 Mass. 827 (1984), and Matter of Schoepfer, 426 Mass. 183 (1997), should apply to the intentional misuse of advance fee payments, and that the committee erred in finding mitigating factors. The board concluded that the presumptive sanctions of those cases should not apply to misuse of fee advances, and recommended the respondent be suspended from the practice of law for three years. An information was filed with the county court, and a single justice of this court ordered the respondent suspended from the practice of law for three years, with the third year stayed. Bar counsel appeals. Following the recently-adopted procedure established by this court for appeals in bar discipline cases, bar counsel has filed a preliminary memorandum (and an amended memorandum). See Order Establishing a Modified Procedure for Appeals in Bar Discipline Cases, Massachusetts Rules of Court 471 (West 2010). Based on our review of the preliminary memoranda and record appendix, and recognizing that the appeal presents an issue similar to an issue presented in Matter of Pudlo, No. SJC-10707, a case reported to the full court by a single justice, we direct the appeal to proceed in the regular course. The two appeals shall be paired for argument. .
