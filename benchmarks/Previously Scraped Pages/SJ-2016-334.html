    08/25/2016 #12 INTERIM ORDER: " On August 17, 2016, I allowed in part the defendant's petition for relief pursuant to G. L. c. 211, § 3, seeking review of a bail order set in the District Court. The order reduced the defendant's bail from $250.00 to $200.00, based on asserted facts suggesting that the defendant has the financial ability to post bail in this amount using the funds accessible from her canteen account, even assuming that she must pay a bail commissioner's fee of $40. See affidavit of Norma Wassel, par. 5. Thus, as to this defendant, the bail issue is moot insofar as her claim is that bail was set without regard to her financial ability to post bail. I am mindful, however, that the defendant's petition raises constitutional issues that are potentially significant and that she has filed a motion to enlarge and modify relief, which appears to seek declaratory relief on behalf of a class of similarly situated defendants. I am not yet persuaded, however, that this case is in a suitable posture to be reserved and reported to the full court, although I am not ruling out that possibility. As drafted, the petition is in the form of an appellate brief, detailing the arguments supporting the defendant's claim that setting bail without regard to a defendant's ability to pay is unconstitutional. It lacks a statement of facts on which the court could rely in making such a determination, ignoring the principle that constitutional issues are not decided in the abstract. Given the potential gravity of the constitutional issue, the defendant is granted leave to file an amended petition within thirty days. The petition should be in the form of a typical pleading not written as an appellate brief, as it is now with numbered factual allegations and identifiable causes of action to which the Commonwealth will be able to file an answer. It will be up to the defendant and her counsel to decide whether, in these circumstances, to seek systemic relief in behalf of a class of similarly situated defendants even though this case is now moot. If the defendant decides to press her constitutional claim, she should consider whether she is an appropriate person with standing to pursue such relief, having a record of numerous failures to appear for hearings and, after reduction of the bail amount, an uncontested ability to post the bail. The amended petition should focus specifically on the terms of the Massachusetts bail statute and how it is being applied by Massachusetts judges, and why the defendant claims that our statute and its application are unconstitutional. A courtesy copy of the pleading should be sent to the Office of the Attorney General as well, so that the Attorney General is aware of it and has an opportunity to be heard if she wishes. After an amended petition is filed, the Commonwealth will have time to file an answer or other responsive pleading, and I will then be in a better position to know whether this issue should be reserved and reported. Both sides will be given an opportunity to be heard on the question of reservation and report before I make a decision on that. If the case is to be reserved and reported, I would also require an adequate record on which the full court can adjudicate the case. Among other things, the parties would be expected to prepare a statement of all agreed facts and exhibits; the failure to agree on the facts needed for a decision could impair the court's ability to decide the case." (Hines, J.)

    11/08/2016 #22 Letter with Certificate of Service to Justice Hines from Atty. Kimberly West Chief, Criminal Bureau and Atty. Genevieve Nadeau Chief, Civil Rights Division saying ... "By order dated August 25, 2016, this Court requested that the Petitioner in the above referenced case provide the Office of the Attorney General with a copy of her Amended Petition for Relief Under G. L. c. 211, § 3, so that the Attorney General would have the opportunity to be heard, noting that the petition "raise[d] constitutional issues that are potentially significant." We thank you for that opportunity. The Amended Petition raises significant questions concerning the constitutional rights of criminal defendants. But its suitability as a vehicle to answer those questions is diminished by other questions about the justiciability of this case, as well as the limited record before the Court. Accordingly, we respectfully submit that the issues raised by this case warrant referral to the Supreme Judicial Court's Standing Advisory Committee on the Rules of Criminal Procedure. We recommend that the Court refer the matter to the Committee on the Rules of Criminal Procedure. We recommend that the Court refer the matter to the Committee with a request that the Committee issue guidance that will ensure that no defendant is detained pending trial solely because he or she lacks the financial resources to post bail. ...In light of all the considerations referenced above - including the important statutory and constitutional questions raised by the Amended Petition, the significant consequences of pretrial detention for all involved, the issues regarding justiciability and the scope of the record, and the benefits of a thorough rulemaking process - the Attorney General suggests that this matter be referred to the Standing Advisory Committee on the Rules of Criminal Procedure to consider the issue of a defendant's ability to pay in setting cash bail pursuant to G. L. c. 278, § 58." ... filed.
