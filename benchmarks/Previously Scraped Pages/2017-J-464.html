    10/30/2017   RE#1: I agree with the respondent that a motion to stay a judgment should be brought in the District Court in the first instance. See M.R.A.P. 6(a). Accordingly, the temporary stay entered on 10/17/17 is continued pending the District Court's disposition of the pending motion to stay docketed by the District Court on 10/17/17. In addition, while I will assume that the respondent is correct in asserting that the District Court does not typically accept filings by fax, given the circumstances of this case where the petitioner purported to file her "Notice of Transfer to Housing Court" form by email and fax one day prior to her scheduled summary process trial and the respondent had notice prior to trial of the petitioner's desire to transfer her summary process case to the Housing Court, I find that the Notice of Transfer form should be docketed in the District Court. While I am without authority to vacate the default judgment that was entered by the District Court on 9/15/17, the District Court should consider the petitioner's Notice of Transfer and reconsider whether a default judgment should have entered when deciding the pending motion to stay. (Rubin, J.) . *Notice/Attest/Nestor, J.
