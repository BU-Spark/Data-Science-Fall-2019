    07/28/2011 #7 ORDER: Appeal allowed to proceed. Lavonrence Perkins appeals from a judgment of a single justice of this court denying his petition for relief under G. L. c. 211, § 3. He was charged, in a criminal complaint in the Dorchester Division of the Boston Municipal Court, with murder. A probable cause hearing was scheduled three times; on each occasion the Commonwealth sought and received, over Perkins's objection, a continuance while it sought an indictment. Perkins filed his G. L. c. 211, § 3, petition after the second continuance, arguing that the Commonwealth's refusal to proceed with the probable cause hearing violates his rights under G. L. c. 276, §§ 35 & 38, and Mass. R. Crim. P. 3(f) and 7(e), and seeking an order that a probable cause hearing be promptly held. The case is now before this court pursuant to S.J.C. Rule 2:21, as amended, 434 Mass. 1301 (2001). Because the probable cause hearing and the trial court's allowances of the Commonwealth's requests for a continuance are interlocutory matters, Perkins must "set forth the reasons why review of the trial court decision cannot adequately be obtained on appeal from any final adverse judgment in the trial court or by other available means." Rule 2:21 (2). He has done so, and we are persuaded that he cannot adequately obtain appellate review of the issue by other means. In addressing the merits of Perkins's petition, the single justice impliedly recognized this as well. The Commonwealth argued, in its opposition to Perkins's petition, that, "in so far as seeks release on personal recognizance, he may seek a bail order from the lower court . . . . Further, if and when he is indicted, he may file motions in the Superior Court seeking relief due to whatever prejudice he claims has accrued by the presentment to the grand jury while the complaint was pending." Neither of these presents an adequate remedy for the deprivation claimed on appeal: the failure to hold a probable cause hearing. Perkins was indicted on May 6, 2011. The issue of the probable cause hearing, as he recognizes, is therefore moot. Perkins, however, has presented ample evidence in the record before us that the issue is a recurring one. It is also one that will likely escape appellate review in the ordinary course of events. As happened here, by the time this court has an opportunity to consider an appeal from a ruling on a defendant's G. L. c. 211, § 3, petition, the relevant events in the District Court will likely have concluded, either by indictment or, if no indictment is forthcoming, a dismissal of the charges. Therefore, where Perkins has no other adequate remedy, and despite the fact that the issue is moot, he may proceed with his appeal in the regular course. Notice sent.
