    07/23/2015 #10 ORDER: The defendants, Nancy M. Needel and Peter Needel, are appealing from a decision of a judge in the Land Court, following a jury-waived trial, that U.S. Bank National Association, as the trustee of the Asset Backed Securities Corporation Home Equity Loan Trust 2002-HEI, holds the defendants' mortgage loan as a successor-in-interest to New Century Mortgage. The plaintiff, Select Portfolio Servicing, is U.S. Bank's agent. After the parties briefed the appeal in the Appeals Court, we transferred the case here on our motion. In their appeal, the defendants argue, as they did in the trial court, that the assignment of the mortgage to the plaintiff was invalid because the assignment was executed in blank. They also argue, as they argued below, that the assignment was invalid because it was made to a non-existent entity, i.e., a trust that had not yet been formed at the time of the assignment. The trial judge rejected the first argument on the basis that the defendants had not been able to show that the assignment was in fact executed in blank. As to the second argument, he concluded that, although the trust had not yet been formed at the time of the assignment, the assignment was clearly executed in anticipation of creation of the trust and was therefore not invalid on that basis. After we transferred the case here, the plaintiff notified the court that it had discovered that the assignment was, in fact, executed in blank. The facts of the case are thus not as the court (or the parties) believed them to be at the time of transfer. The changed posture of the case makes it uncertain whether the appellate court deciding the appeal will reach the issue whether an assignment of a mortgage loan to a trustee of a trust that does not yet exist, but that is formed thereafter, constitutes a valid assignment. We are not inclined to decide that issue unnecessarily. In light of the changed facts, we are remanding the case to the Appeals Court. The court is free to decide the appeal in its current posture or, if it deems appropriate, remand the matter to the Land Court in order for the trial judge to address in the first instance the effect of the execution of the assignment in blank. If the matter is remanded to the Land Court, either side remains free to seek direct appellate review in this court following the proceedings on remand. By the Court.
    07/23/2015   Case transferred to Appeals Court.
    08/05/2015 #11 MOTION for Clarification of July 23, 2015, Order, filed for Nancy M. Needel & another by Gordon N. Schultz, Esquire. **(8/10/15) This case has been remanded to the Appeals Court. The Appeals Court will decide whether the case ought to be remanded to the Land Court, and, if so, the timing, scope, and terms of the remand. This court's order was not intended to define the Appeals Court's power in that regard. Any questions or motions concerning a possible remand should be addressed to the Appeals Court.
