    01/26/2010 #13 ORDER (By the Court): The parties, including the Commissioner of Revenue as amicus curiae, are hereby directed to file supplemental memoranda or briefs, not to exceed ten pages of legal argument, on or before February 10, 2010, addressing the following questions: (1) Where it has been held that the Commissioner lacked authority to centrally value the taxpayer's property, see Bell Atlantic Mobile of Mass. Copr., Ltd. v. Commissioner of Revenue, 451 Mass. 280 (2008), whether the plaintiff municipalities may now assess the taxpayer's property for the years in question pursuant to G.L.c. 59, sec. 77. (2) In the unique circumstances of this case, where the municipalities' appeals to the Appellate Tax Board under G.L.c. 59, sec. 39, were dismissed without a determination ever having been made whether the Commissioner had undervalued the taxpayers' property, whether the court has the power in equity to order the appeals reinstated for a decision by the board on that aspect of the case or otherwise to authorize the municipalities to make their own assessments.
