    10/17/2017 #6 ORDER: Final judgment originally entered in this matter on August 14, 2014. The trial judge's memorandum and order specified that judgment should enter by establishment of four specific liens, one on each of four different identified condominium units, each of which was owned by one of the defendant Trusts. The judgment itself was carefully crafted, stating only that the plaintiffs had established each of the four specified liens. The defendants appealed, and this court affirmed the judgment on January 27, 2016. On May 9, 2016, the plaintiff, Trustees of the Washington West Condominium, filed a Motion for Judgment After Rescript. The defendants opposed the motion arguing inter alia that the plaintiff's proposed judgment, which among other things was in the form of a simple money judgment, awarded relief against defendant Ashkouri individually, improperly expanding the relief awarded in the original judgment affirmed on appeal. The motion judge, who was not the trial judge, allowed the Motion for Judgment After Rescript, and on May 25, 2016, entered a Judgment After Rescript, not in the form of the judgment as entered by the trial judge, but as a wholly rewritten money judgment apparently against all defendants, including Ashkouri individually. Twelve days later, on June 6, 2016, defendant Ashkouri, Trustee of Ashkouri Realty Trust filed an "Emergency Motion for Reconsideration and Request for Hearing on Defendants' Motion for Judgment After Rescript." The plaintiffs characterize this as an untimely filed motion under Rule 59(e) to amend the Judgment After Rescript. It might instead be characterized as a motion for relief from the Judgment After Rescript under Rule 60, but the difference is immaterial. In any event, eleven days later, on June 17, 2016, the defendants filed a Notice of Appeal from the Order entering Judgment After Rescript. It was filed within 30 days of the Order, but before the Emergency Motion was ruled upon on June 27, 2016. On that date, the Emergency Motion was denied for failure to comply with Superior Court Rule 9A. The clerk did not docket, and has not docketed, the Notice of Appeal filed on June 17, 2016. On March 28, 2017, after counsel learned the Notice of Appeal had not been docketed, defendant Ashkouri, Trustee of Ashkouri Realty Trust filed a motion to correct the Judgment After Rescript, or to docket the Notice of Appeal from June 17, 2016. In an affidavit accompanying the filing counsel for the defendant swore that "The Trial Court clerk called me upon receipt of the Notice of Appeal stating that I could not appeal and I stated that I could because the Judgment changed after the first Appeal." Counsel further averred that he had learned the Notice of Appeal had not been docketed and asked that, in the absence of action to correct the Judgment After Rescript, it be docketed. That Motion was denied on September 5, 2017, and the instant petition followed. I requested an opposition from the plaintiff/respondent, which has now been filed. The plaintiff/respondent argues that the Emergency Motion tolled the time for filing the Notice of Appeal, and the Notice of Appeal was prematurely filed. Since 2013, a motion under Rule 59 or a motion under Rule 60 filed and served within ten days of an order or judgment from which the party seeks to appeal will toll the time for filing a notice of appeal. Mass. R. App. P. 4(a). A motion filed or served after that time will not. Id. The Certificate of Service accompanying the Emergency Motion, which the parties did not submit but which I have obtained from the Trial Court, indicates that the Emergency Motion was served on June 3, 2016.[1] Thus, the Emergency Motion was timely filed and served. (The plaintiff/respondent states that it was filed beyond the ten-day period--in which case it would not have tolled the time for filing the Notice of Appeal--but I take judicial notice of the calendar, which shows the tenth day after the judgment after rescript fell on a Saturday, and the Emergency Motion was filed the following Monday.) So, the Emergency Motion tolled the time period for filing the Notice of Appeal, which was a nullity when filed. The defendants thus were required to file a new notice of appeal within 30 days of June 27, 2016 in order to appeal the May 25, 2016, Judgment After Rescript. Within one year of that date, in light of the clerk's statement, however, they filed their March 28, 2017 motion, which was not denominated a new notice of appeal, but a motion to docket the earlier notice of appeal. Given the circumstances, I will treat the March 28, 2017 filing as a late-filed notice of appeal from the Judgment after Rescript, which I will deem timely nunc pro tunc (Rubin, J.).
