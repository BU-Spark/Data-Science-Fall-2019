    08/01/2017 #20 ORDER: This matter is before this court only because the United States Court of Appeals for the First Circuit certified to us a single question of Massachusetts law that is important to the resolution of an appeal pending in the Court of Appeals (No. 16-1254). That court retains jurisdiction of the underlying appeal while the certified question (SJC 12247) is pending before us. The parties have filed two motions to stay one in the Court of Appeals and one in this court seeking temporarily to stay the respective proceedings while they seek approval of a proposed class settlement in the Federal District Court. In an order dated July 24, 2017, the Court of Appeals declined to act on the motion filed in that court, and appeared to defer to this court to act first on the motion to stay that has been filed in this court. Although we appreciate such deference, we do not think it appropriate in an appeal that is only before us because of a certified question from a sister court. As long as there remains a certified question before us from the Court of Appeals, we will decide the question unless the Court of Appeals requests that we stay our answer or withdraws the question from our consideration. We therefore decline to act on the parties' motion to stay and will proceed to answer the question, unless and until we receive direction from the Court of Appeals to do otherwise. If the Court of Appeals stays the underlying appeal, we will stay the matter before us and hold the certified question in abeyance until the Federal stay is lifted and we are asked to proceed; if the underlying appeal is eventually dismissed following approval of a settlement and the certified question is therefore withdrawn, or if the question is withdrawn at any time for any other reason, we will not answer the question. Absent a stay of the underlying appeal or withdrawal of the question, we shall proceed to answer the question. In addition to notifying the parties of this order, the Clerk of this court shall also furnish the Clerk of the Court of Appeals with a copy of the order.
