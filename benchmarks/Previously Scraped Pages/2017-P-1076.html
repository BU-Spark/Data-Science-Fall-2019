    10/27/2017 #7 Decision: Rule 1:28 The judgment of the Probate and Family Court dated September 29, 2015, is reversed. The matter is remanded to that court where a decree is to enter forthwith incorporating the following numbered findings: 1. Orlando was born on November 7, 1996, in El Salvador and currently lives with his father in East Boston, Suffolk County, Massachusetts. 2. The Probate and Family Court has jurisdiction pursuant to G. L. c. 215, § 6, to make equity determinations within the meaning of § 101(a)(27)(J) of the INA, 8 U.S.C. § 1101(a)(27)(J)(i) (2012), and 8 C.F.R. § 204.11(a) & (c) (2015). Orlando is dependent upon the Probate and Family Court and under its jurisdiction relative to these proceedings. 3. Reunification of Orlando with his mother is not viable due to abuse, neglect, abandonment, or similar grounds under State law. 4. It is not in the best interests of Orlando to return to El Salvador, his country of nationality, and it is in his best interests to remain in the United States. The First Justice of the Suffolk Division of the Probate and Family Court shall direct the Register to enter the decree also forthwith, but in any event no later than November 1, 2017. The rescript shall issue forthwith. (Green, Blake, Lemire, JJ.). *Notice.

    10/31/2017 #8 PETITION for Rehearing filed for Orlando Javier Hernandez Tejada by Attorney Elizabeth Badger.
    11/01/2017   RE#8: The petition for rehearing of the plaintiff, having been considered, is denied. The memorandum and order issued October 27, 2017, is hereby amended as follows: on page five, the numbered paragraphs, 1 - 4, are deleted and the following paragraphs numbered 1 - 5 are inserted in their place: 1. The plaintiff, Orlando Javier Hernandez Tejada, was born on November 7, 1996, in El Salvador. Orlando resides in the East Boston neighborhood of the city of Boston, Suffolk County, Massachusetts in the care of his father and under the jurisdiction of the Probate and Family Court. Orlando is not married. 2. 2. The Probate and Family Court has jurisdiction pursuant to G. L. c. 215, § 6, to make determinations about the dependency, care, support, and well-being of youth under the age of twenty-one. See Eccleston v. Bankosky, 438 Mass. 428, 436-437 (2003). Orlando Javier Hernandez Tejada is dependent upon the Probate and Family Court for his health, safety, and welfare. Ibid. See G. L. c. 216, § 6 (Probate and Family Court has broad equity powers). 3. Orlando's mother, the named defendant, neglected and abandoned Orlando by failing to seek police assistance to ensure his safety, refusing to relocate to a less dangerous neighborhood, and providing him with no support in removing himself from the dangers of the community. For these reasons, Orlando's reunification with his mother, Cristina Elida Tejada Lemus, is not viable due to neglect and abandonment. See 110 Code Mass. Regs. § 2.00 (2008). 4. Having considered the health, educational, developmental, physical and emotional interests of Orlando, this court determines that it is not in Orlando's best interest to return to his country of nationality, El Salvador. See, e.g., Custody of Kali, 439 Mass. 834, 843-845 (2003) (in making a best interests determination, a judge must identify and weigh the pertinent factors). In El Salvador, Orlando would lack parental protection and support and would be at risk of physical violence. In the United States, Orlando has found stability, safety, and education that he could not access in El Salvador. For these reasons, it is in Orlando's best interests to remain in the United States under the care of his father, Mr. Jaime Hernandez Carcamo. 5. The above findings were made due to the abandonment and neglect of Orlando by his mother, the defendant, to provide for his safety and well-being, and to protect Orlando from future harm, in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. A copy of this order, the revised decision, and revised rescript cover are sent this date to the Probate and Family Court, counsel and the Office of the Reporter of Decisions. (Green, Blake, Lemire, JJ.)[1] *Notice/Attest Footnote 1. The panelists are listed in order of seniority.
