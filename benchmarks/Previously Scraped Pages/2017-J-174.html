    08/31/2017 #6 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER: The plaintiff has filed a petition, pursuant to G. L. c. 231, § 118 (first para.) seeking review of the Superior Court's denial of a motion to amend the complaint to add another physician who treated the decedent. The defendants filed an opposition to the petition, and proceedings on the petition were stayed pending the resolution of a motion to reconsider in the Superior Court. The Superior Court has denied the motion for reconsideration. The stay is therefore vacated. To succeed on the petition, the plaintiff must demonstrate that the Superior Court judge committed a clear error of law or an abuse of discretion. See Jet-Line Services, Inc. v. Board of Selectmen of Stoughton, 25 Mass. App. Ct. 645, 646 (1988). It does not appear from the record before me that there was a clear error of law. Nor can it be said that the judge made a clear error of judgment in weighing the various factors, including the timeliness of the proposed amendment and whether there is support for a claim against Dr. Munzo. See L.L. v. Commonwealth, 470 Mass. 169, 185 n.27 (2014) ("An appellate court's review of a trial judge's decision for abuse of discretion must give great deference to the judge's exercise of discretion; it is plainly not an abuse of discretion simply because a reviewing court would have reached a different result"). Therefore, the petition is denied. (Vuono, J.). *Notice/Attest/Dupuis, J Footnotes: 1. Anna Daponte. 2. Jaime McGarrahan, Lisa M.
