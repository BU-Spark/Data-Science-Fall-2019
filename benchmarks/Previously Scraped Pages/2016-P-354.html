    05/03/2017 #15 ORDER: (RE#12) In accordance with our decision in the underlying case issued pursuant to Appeals Court Rule 1:28, the defendant, Sandra Staub, filed an application for attorney's fees in the total amount of $4,920.00 and double costs of $40.38 for a total of $4,960.38. Where the defendant has prevailed on its claim and awarded fees, the defendant's petition for fees must be submitted "together with the necessary back-up material and details as to hours spent, precise nature of the work, and fees requested," Yorke Management v. Castro, 406 Mass. 17, 20 (1989), so as to provide this court with a reasonable basis upon which to assess whether the time expended was reasonable. In our determination of the reasonable appellate fees, which is a "largely discretionary" function, we do not act as "a mere calculator of numbers, but properly exercise[] independent judgment concerning the request's reasonableness." Stowe v. Bologna, 417 Mass. 199, 204 (1994). We approach the assessment of fees using the lodestar method, which involves "multiplying the number of hours reasonably spent on the case times a reasonable hourly rate." Fontaine v. Ebtec Corp., 415 Mass. 309, 324 (1993). "[T]he focus is not the bill submitted [] or the amount in controversy [] but several factors, including 'the nature of the case and the issues presented, the time and labor required, the amount of damages involved, the result obtained, the experience, reputation and ability of the attorney, the usual price charged for similar services by other attorneys in the same area, and the amount of awards in similar cases.'" Berman v. Linnane, 434 Mass. 301, 303 (2001), quoting from Linthicum v. Archambault, 379 Mass. 381, 388-389, (1979). We are "not required to review and allow or disallow each individual item in the bill, but [may] consider the bill as a whole." Ibid. After review of the fee petition, and the plaintiff's response thereto, and in consideration of the original briefs and the record, the time expended, the relative complexity of the issues, the level of expertise and experience required and the fees customarily charged for such work, we are of the opinion that the fee requests reflect efficient litigation.[3] The defendant's application for attorney's fees is allowed in the amount of $4,920.00 and double costs of $40.38 for a total of $4,960.38. Any proceedings to enforce this order shall be commenced in the Trial Court. (Trainor, Massing & Desmond, JJ.[4]) *Notice/Attest Footnotes [1] Individually and on behalf of the estate of Gregory Gillespie. [2] Peggy Roggenbuck Gillespie and David Angier.
