    05/08/2017 #1 Motion for stay under M.R.A.P. 6(a) with attachments, filed by Michael Camacho.
    05/11/2017 #2 ORDER: The petition is untimely, as the order was docketed in the Superior Court on March 31, 2017, and the petition filed on May 8, 2017. General Laws c. 231, § 118, par. 1, provides that a petition for relief may be filed "within thirty days of the entry of such order."[1] "Entry" is the date the order or action is entered on the docket of the trial court. See GD Mathews & Sons v. MSN Corp., 54 Mass. App. Ct. 18, 24-26 (2002); Standard Register Co. v. Bolton-Emerson, Inc., 35 Mass. App. Ct. 570, 571-572 (1993). The thirty day time limit is jurisdictional, and the single justice is without authority to entertain a petition that is untimely. See McGrath v.McGrath, 65 Mass. App. Ct. 670, 672-673 (2006). Accordingly, the petition is dismissed (Green, J.). *Notice/Attest/Dupuis, J Footnotes: 1. The petitioner asserts that the order "issued" on April 3, 2017, but it is the date of entry, not of "issuance," that determines timeliness. In any event, the petition was not filed within thirty days of April 3, 2017.
