    08/04/2008 #6 ANNOUNCEMENT: The Justices are soliciting amicus briefs. The issues presented are whether claims that the plaintiffs' subdivision filings were entitled to "freeze" protection under G. L. c. 40A, s. 6, or G. L. c. 111, s. 127P, were moot or nonjusticiable, where plaintiffs had not timely appealed from the town's denial of certificates of constructive approval; whether plaintiffs' challenges to the Aquinnah and Building Cap Districts of Critical Planning Concern were untimely; whether plaintiff trustees, non-lawyers, may appear pro se on behalf of the trusts where they have not demonstrated that they are the sole beneficiaries. Argument is tentatively scheduled for November 2008. Amicus submissions are due on or before October 20, 2008. *Notice.

    02/20/2009 #13 RESCRIPT (Full Opinion): There was no error in the Superior Court judge's allowance of the defendants' motions for summary judgment. The judgment below shall be modified to declare that the plaintiffs are not entitled to an eight-year zoning freeze under G.L. c. 40A, s6, fifth par., or a three-year freeze of board of health regulations under G.L. c. 111, s127P. As so modified, the judgment is affirmed. (By the Court) Reasons as on file. Notice sent.
