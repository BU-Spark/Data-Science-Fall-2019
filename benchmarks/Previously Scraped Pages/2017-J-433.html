    12/12/2017 #11 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER: This matter came before the Single Justice on a petition to waive a summary process appeal bond pursuant to M.G.L. c. 239 sec. 5. On August 31, 2017 after a jury trial in the Housing Court, judgment entered for the plaintiff for possession and damages in the amount of $12,600.00. Additionally, judgment entered for the plaintiff on defendant's claims for breach of warranty of habitability, interference with quiet enjoyment and violation of 93A. The defendant's timely motion to waive an appeal bond was denied by the trial court, and an appeal bond in the amount of $7,000.00 and the payment of reasonable use and occupancy in the amount of $1,400.00 a month was ordered on September 19, 2017. On November 27, 2017 the trial court denied reconsideration. A hearing before the Single Justice took place on December 11, 2017. The plaintiff and his counsel appeared. Defendant's counsel appeared without the defendant. The defendant contends that she has a defense that is not frivolous. After hearing argument and reviewing the filings, the order of the Housing Court is affirmed. In reaching this conclusion, I find that the defendant has not established she has a non-frivolous defense or that the amount of use and occupancy is unreasonable. In so deciding I reviewed the affidavit of indigency filed by the defendant. Since the defendant chose not to appear at the Single Justice hearing, the court did not have the opportunity to inquire of her ability to pay, and based on arguments regarding her ability to pay presented by the plaintiff, I find the affidavit to be self-serving and incomplete. The trial court order denying the defendant's motion to waive appeal bond is affirmed. So Ordered. (Lemire, J.). Notice/Attest/Muirhead, J.
