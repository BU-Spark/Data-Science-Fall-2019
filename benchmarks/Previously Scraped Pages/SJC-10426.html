    06/29/2009 #12 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER (By the Court): The defendant has moved for the payment of appellate counsel fees pursuant to M.R.Crim.P. 15(d), as appearing in 422 Mass. 1501 (1996). His certificate of service to the motion reflects service made only to the Commonwealth. Pursuant to Comm. v. Phinney, 448 Mass. 621, 625 & n.5 (1007), the Administrative Office of the Trial Corut (AOTC) should have been served with the request, and should be given an opportunity to be heard. Accordingly, it is hereby ORDERED that the defendant serve a copy of his motion (and all attachments), as well as a copy of the Commonwealth's opposition (and all attachments) and a copy of this order, forthwith on the AOTC. Service shall be made no later than Friday, July 3, 2009. The AOTC shall have until Friday, July 17, 2009, to file a response with the court (and effectuate service). (Notice to counsel and AOTC.)

    07/29/2009 #17 ORDER (By the Court): Appellate defense counsel, David J. Nathanson, has requested an award of appellate attorney's fees and costs in the amount of $23,450. Pursuant to MRCrimP 15(d)...appellate counsel for the defendant is entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees and costs for work in this case responding to the Commonwealth's appeal from the allowance, in the Superior Court, of the defendant's motion to suppress evidence. Appellate defense counsel timely filed a motion for attorney's fees and costs, an affidavit setting forth counsel's hourly rate ($200 per hour), seven affidavits of attorneys who practice criminal law in the Commonwealth attesting to the reasonableness of this rate, and a detailed accounting of the number of hours spent working on the appeal (80.25 hours). He later filed a supplemental motion for payment, which sought reimbursement for 15.25 additional hours of his time. Part of his submission was an accounting of time spent by his associate, Dan A. Horowitz, assisting in the preparation of the defendant's brief to this court (43.5 hours at an hourly rate of $100). Appellate defense counsel asserts that he does not request payment for costs. The Commonwealth has filed an opposition, asserting that the single issue raised by the defendant's appeal required only a portion of the time for which appellate counsel has requested payment, because counsel who represented the defendant at the motion hearing (not his appellate counsel) filed a brief that succeeded in the trial court. The Commonwealth also asserts that payment should not be made for time spent in connection with the arguments raised in Comm. v. Murphy, a case argued before the court the same day, which were not raised below by the defendant. The Commonwealth points out, correctly, that appellate counsel's request is far higher than the average rule 15(d) award, see Comm. v. Ennis, 441 Mass. 718, 723 (2004) and suggests that one-third the requested fees, or $7000, is reasonable. We acknowledge the broad public interest of the arguments asserted in the defendant's appellate brief and acknowledge as well that appellate defense counsel was requested to complete the brief on an expedited schedule. Taking into consideration the factors outlined in MRProfC 1.5(a)...we conclude that appellate defense counsel's hourly rate of $200 is reasonable, as is the $100 hourly rate requested for the work done by his associate. While we do not agree with the Commonwealth's characterization of the appeal as presenting "a simple issue," we conclude that 139 hours is an unreasonable amount of time to spend researching and briefing the issue on appeal. After examining with care the submitted time sheets, we conclude that the time spent preparing the defense in this case, including almost twenty-four hours spent researching constitutional history (e.g., art. 12 and the works of John Adams) and the approximately forty hours spent drafting the brief, exceeded what was reasonable. Accordingly, the defendant's request is allowed in an amount reflecting reasonable hours expended on the Commonwealth's appeal. The defendant is awarded $12,500 in attorney's fees. See Berman v. Linnane, 434 Mass. 301, 303 (2001). This order shall be enforced in all respects by the Brockton Division of the District Court Department. (Notice to counsel and Brockton District Court.) (8/4/09 - AMENDED; previous language of last sentence: ...by the Plymouth County Superior Court.)
