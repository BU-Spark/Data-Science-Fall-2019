    08/01/2017 #1 Appeal entered pursuant to M.G.L.c. 239, § 5 with attachments.
    08/02/2017   ORDER: On July 24, 2017, the Appeals Court clerk's office received a transmission of assembly from the Worcester Housing Court. It is unclear from that transmission whether the matter was transmitted pursuant to G. L. c. 239, § 5(g) (single justice review of the amount of use and occupancy to be paid during the pendency of the appeal) or if it was transmitted pursuant to Mass. R. A. P. 9(d) (panel review of the final judgment in the summary process action) thus triggering the appellant's obligation to docket the appeal pursuant to Mass. R. A. P. 10(a)(1). The matter was entered on the single justice docket of the Appeals Court on August 1, 2017. On or before August 9, 2017, the parties are to inform this court, in writing, of their understanding as to whether this matter is here to be reviewed by a single justice on the issue of the amount of the use and occupancy payments or whether the defendant is now required to docket the appeal from the judgment. *Notice

    09/12/2017 #5 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER: The defendant has appealed from the judgment for possession subsequent to a summary process action. The defendant is a two-thirds owner of the subject property. The plaintiff sought to establish the payment of use and occupancy during the pendency of the appeal. A judge of the Worcester Housing Court allowed the plaintiff's motion and ordered the defendant to pay $1000 per month as use and occupancy. In her order, the judge stated that the order of payment for use and occupancy was not a bond order as it was the plaintiff who initiated the proceedings. Nevertheless, the defendant sought review of the order through the procedures set forth in the summary process statute for review of an appeals bond. G. L. c. 239, § 5(f)-(g). The Housing Court clerk's office transmitted an assembly which included the use and occupancy order, and the case was entered on the single justice docket for review. Upon review, of the docket it appears that the use and occupancy order is the functional equivalent of an appeal bond and is thus subject to review. After a hearing, the order dated 5/15/17 is remanded to the Housing Court for clarification as to whether the amount required is the full value of use and occupancy notwithstanding the fact that it appears that the defendant remains a two-thirds owner of the subject property and whether the minority owner could be made whole following the sale of the property if the judgment is affirmed. (Vuono, J.). *Notice/Attest/Horan, J.
    09/21/2017 #6 Copy of clarification of this court's order regarding use and occupancy payments as requested by the plaintiff, received from Housing Ct, Worcester Cty.
    10/06/2017 #7 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER Upon review of the Housing Court Judge's clarification order, the judge's order that the defendant pay use and occupancy of $1000 month is affirmed as there is no reason evident in the record before me that the amount ordered is erroneous. Further, the defendant may argue that she is entitled to all or part of any funds paid pursuant to the bond order at the conclusion of her appeal regardless of the outcome of the appeal. (Vuono, J.). Notice/attest/Horan, J.
