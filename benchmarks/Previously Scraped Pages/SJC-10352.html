The issue presented, among others, is: in the case of a freeze-out in a closely held corporation, should the rule of Brodie v. Jordan, 447 Mass. 866 (2006), be modified to allow a buy-out or some other remedy, in circumstances in which the corporation became insolvent during the pendency of litigation, so that the freeze-out plaintiff was left with no effective remedy under Brodie. Argument is tentatively scheduled for May 2009. Amicus submissions are due on or before April 20, 2009.

    12/31/2009 #19 RESCRIPT (Full Opinion): The decision of the trial court judge is affirmed insofar as he found for Pointer on his claims and on the defendants' counterclaims. The denial of Pointer's motion for relief from judgment that requested a forced buyout of his shares of FGC is affirmed. The case is remanded for further proceedings consistent with this opinion concerning an appropriate remedy for the freeze-out. (By the Court).
