    04/02/2009 #3 ORDER (By the Court): The defendant appeals from a judgment of a single justice of this court granting a petition filed by the Commonwealth pursuant to GLc211, s3. The judgment vacated an interlocutory order by a judge in the Superior Court in the underlying criminal case that would have required the Commonwealth to disclose the identity of two informants. The appeal is presently before us on a memorandum and record appendix filed by the defendant, which, it appears, he submitted in an attempt to comply with SJC Rule 2:21, as amended, 434 Mass. 1301 (2001). That rule applies only in cases where a single justice denies relief from a challenged interlocutory ruling in the trial court...and not where a single justice grants relief from an interlocutory ruling. The rule does not apply here. The defendant is thus free to proceed with his appeal in the ordinary course. The timetable for briefing will commence on the date of this order. We express no view at this juncture whether the Commonwealth was entitled to extraordinary review and relief pursuant to GLc211, s3. The threshold question as to the appropriateness of the single justice's reviewing the matter under GLc211, s3, as well as the correctness of his granting relief on the merits of the petition, shall be addressed by the parties in their briefs. Notice to counsel.
