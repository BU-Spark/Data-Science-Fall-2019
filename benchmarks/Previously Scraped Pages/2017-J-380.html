Action on this case is stayed pending entry of a final determination on the motion to waive the entry fee. Notice Sent
    08/25/2017 #2 Petition pursuant to G.L. c. 231, § 118 with attachments, filed by Warren C. Tyler.
    09/01/2017 #3 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER: The plaintiff has filed a petition pursuant to G. L. c. 231, § 118 requiring him to post the full bond pursuant to G. L. c. 231, § 60B. To succeed in his petition, the plaintiff must show that the challenged order is the product of a clear error of law or an abuse of the judge' s discretion. See Jet-Line Services, Inc. v. Board of Selectmen of Stoughton, 25 Mass. App. Ct. 645, 646 (1988). Based upon the plaintiff's speculative theories of duty and breach and the lack of expert evidence to support the plaintiff's offer of proof, neither the decision to require the bond nor the decision to deny the plaintiff's request to reduce it based on his indigency are abuses of discretion. See Faircloth v. DiLillo, 466 Mass. 120, 124 (2013). Therefore, the petition is denied. (Vuono, J.). *Notice/Attest/Wilkins, J. Footnotes: 1.
