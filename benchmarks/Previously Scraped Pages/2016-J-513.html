    12/07/2016   Case entered without fee per Kinder, J. - see order of 12/07/2016
    12/07/2016 #1 Motion for stay under M.R.A.P. 6(a) with attachments, filed by Steven A. Wilbur.
    12/07/2016 #2 ORDER: The defendant in a summary process case has filed a motion to stay an eviction. The motion was filed the morning that the eviction was scheduled to occur. The defendant's appeal from the summary process judgment was dismissed for failure to comply with an appeal bond order. The bond order was issued by the Housing Court and affirmed by a single justice of this Court. The defendant's pending appeal is from the dismissal of his appeal from the judgment, and limited to the whether the bond order was appropriate. See Matter of an Appeal Bond (No. 1), 428 Mass. 1013 (1998); Erickson v. Somers, 446 Mass. 1015 (2006); Ford v. Braman, 30 Mass. App. Ct. 968, 970 (1991). Contrary to Rule 6 of the Massachusetts Rules of Appellate Procedure, the motion to stay was not filed in the first instance in the trial court. Although it is arguably impracticable at this late hour, the appeal was dismissed on 10/19/16, and the stay could have been sought any time thereafter. Further, the defendant's motion was dropped off at the clerk's office without the necessary filing fee or motion to waive the filing fee. Notwithstanding these defects, the motion is hereby entered on the docket of this Court without the need for payment of fee, and I have reviewed it on the merits. The defendant has failed to demonstrate a sufficient likelihood of success on the merits of his appeal. See C.E. v. J.E., 472 Mass. 1016, 1017 (2015). Although the Housing Court judge's finding that a bond set pursuant to G.L. c. 239, § 6 cannot be waived is error, that error is harmless in light of the judge's other findings, as affirmed by the single justice of this Court. Home Savings Bank of America, FSB v. Camillo, 45 Mass. App. Ct. 910, 911 (1998)("Section 6 of c. 239 spells out what damages a bond shall protect in the case of a summary process action arising out of mortgage foreclosure. It does not cause the procedures of s. 5 to be inapplicable in such cases.") Specifically, the Housing Court judge found that the defendant would not be entitled to waiver of the bond as the defendant is neither indigent, nor in possession of any non-frivolous defenses to the eviction. The defendant's emergency motion for stay does not address why the Housing Court judge's finding that he is not indigent was error. The defendant's argument that he has meritorious issues on appeal is conclusory at best. For these reasons, the defendant's motion to stay levy on the execution on the judgment for possession in Worcester Housing Court docket number 16H85SP895 is denied. (Kinder, J.). *Notice/Attest/Horan, J. Footnotes: 1. Daniel Wilbur and Michele D. Wilbur
