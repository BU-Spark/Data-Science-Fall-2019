    04/24/2017 #9 ORDER: Because of a question regarding the timeliness of the plaintiff, Mr. Reade's, attempt to enter this case, and with knowledge of the Supreme Judicial Court's decision in Reade v. Secretary of the Commonwealth, 472 Mass. 573, 585-586 (2015), concerning Mr. Reade's previous motion for fees and costs, including the facts recited therein, an assistant clerk referred Mr. Reade's motion to waive the entry fee for his appeal and supporting affidavit of indigency to the single justice. See Reade v. Secretary of the Commonwealth, 472 Mass. 573, 585-586 (2015) (prior proceedings involving the party moving for waiver of court fees may raise significant questions regarding an otherwise regular and complete affidavit of indigency, thus warranting a referral of the request to a judge for determination in the first instance). A hearing on the plaintiff's motion to waive the entry fee was scheduled for March 29, 2017. The plaintiff notified the Court in a letter accompanying his docketing statement that he would be out of state until April 5. The Court sua sponte rescheduled the hearing. Notice was sent to the plaintiff on April 10 that the hearing had been rescheduled. Contrary to his assertion in his April 14 letter, the notice was sent to the correct address, as demonstrated by the copy of the notice attached to that letter. Nevertheless, the plaintiff did not appear at the hearing on April 13, 2017. On April 14 and April 18, the court received letters from Mr. Reade regarding his motion to waive, his address, and the hearing. The plaintiff's motion to waive his filing fee is denied for the reasons set forth in Reade, 472 Mass. at 580-585. If the plaintiff does not pay the required fee on or before May 4, 2017, entry of the appeal on this Court's docket shall be vacated. The plaintiff may appeal this decision by filing a notice of appeal within the time provided by G.L. c. 261, s. 27D, i.e., within 7 days of receipt of this notice. (Carhart, J.). *Notice
