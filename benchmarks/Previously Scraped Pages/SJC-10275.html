    10/30/2008 #3 ORDER (By the Court): Petitioner Jery Campagna was tried on 7 indictments alleging conspiracy to obtain a controlled substance by fraud, and an 8th indictment alleging theft of a controlled substance. At the close of the evidence, the judge granted the petitioner's reserved motion for severance of the 8th indictment, and declared a mistrial as to it. He also ruled the Commonwealth had presented sufficient evidence of one overarching conspiracy, that indictments 1 through 7 were duplicative, and therefore entered required findings of not guilty on indictments 2 through 7. After the case was submitted to the jury on the sole remaining conspiracy indictment, the jury deadlocked, and a mistrial was declared. The trial judge thereafter denied the defendant's motion to dismiss indictments 1 through 8 on the ground of double jeopardy. The petitioner filed a ... petition in the county court challenging the denial of his motion to dismiss, which was denied by a single justice of this court. Following the procedure of SJC Rule 2:21..., the petitioner appealed. Because the Superior Court order challenged by the petitioner is interlocutory, rule 2:21 requires him to state "the reasons why review of the trial court decision cannot adequately be obtained on appeal from any final adverse judgment in the trial court or by other available means.".... The petitioner's motion to dismiss is grounded in the claim that retrial will violate his right against double jeopardy. Without addressing the substantive merits of the appeal, in such circumstances we have permitted interlocutory review ... because "appellate review ... after trial and conviction would not provide adequate relief if the defendant were to prevail on the double jeopardy issue after trial." ... The petitioner may pursue his appeal pursuant to the regular appellate process. (Notice to counsel.)
