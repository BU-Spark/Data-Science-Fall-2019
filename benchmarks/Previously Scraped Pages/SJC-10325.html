    12/19/2008 #5 ORDER (By the Court): The respondent, Manuel Narea, is charged in the BMC with distribution of cocaine (GLc94C, s32A) and a school zone violation (GLc94C, s32J). He appeals from a judgment of a single justice of this court granting the Commonwealth's petition pursuant to GLc211, s3, and vacating a pretrial ruling by a judge in the trial court that would have precluded the Commonwealth from introducing testimony at trial regarding the alleged "buy money." The respondent has filed in the full court a document entitled "Petition to the Full Bench of the Supreme Judicial Court to Vacate the Order of the Single Justice Under GLc211, s3," which he has indicated in a letter to the court clerk, "was intended to be filed under SJC Rule 2:21." We therefore treat his submission as a memorandum pursuant to SJC Rule 2:21, as amended, 434 Mass. 1301 (2001). That rule applies, however, only in cases where "a single justice denies relief from a challenged interlocutory ruling in the trial court"..., and not in cases where the single justice has granted a petition for relief from an interlocutory ruling. The rule thus does not apply here. The respondent may proceed with his appeal in the ordinary course. The briefing timetable commences on the date of this order; no extensions of time for briefing should be anticipated by either side. The case is to be scheduled for argument at the court's April, 2009 sitting. We express no view at this juncture whether the Commonwealth was entitled to extraordinary review and relief pursuant to GLc211, s3. The threshold question as to the appropriateness of the single justice's reviewing the matter under GLc211, s3, as well as the correctness of his granting relief on the merits on the case, are to be addressed by the parties in their brief. (Notice to counsel.)
