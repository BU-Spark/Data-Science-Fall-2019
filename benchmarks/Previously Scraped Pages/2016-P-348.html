    12/30/2016   RESCRIPT to Trial Court.
    01/06/2017 #26 ORDER: (RE#22) In our memorandum and order dated November 2, 2016, we allowed the request by plaintiff-appellee Robert O'Regan, as guardian and conservator of Alice Migell, for attorney's fees and costs incurred in defending these appeals. O'Regan having subsequently filed an appropriately-supported request, along with a motion to apportion the award between the equity and criminal contempt portions of the appeal, and the defendant-appellants having filed no opposition or other response, we rule as follows. We calculate attorney fees according to the lodestar method, "multiplying the number of hours reasonably spent on this case times a reasonable hourly rate." Fontaine v. Ebtec Corp., 415 Mass. 309, 324 (1993). First, we consider the number of hours reasonably spent on this case. When analyzing hours, we may disallow or reduce certain time expended. Cf. City Rentals, LLC v. BBC Co., Inc., 79 Mass. App. Ct. 559, 567 (2011) ("Reasonable fees and costs for a prevailing party do not include compensation for unnecessary or ineffectual activity. The fees seeker must show the utility of the services"). We note that the plaintiff-appellee did not prevail on one of the six principal issues presented on appeal, and we therefore reduce his attorneys' billed hours by fifteen percent, to 102.6 hours. Second, we determine a reasonable hourly rate. "In determining the amount of a reasonable fee, we consider 'the nature of the case and the issues presented, the time and labor required, the amount of damages involved, the result obtained, the experience, reputation and ability of the attorney, the usual price charged for similar services by other attorneys in the same area, and the amount of awards in similar cases.'" Haddad v. Wal-Mart Stores, Inc., 455 Mass. 1024, 1025 (2010), quoting from Linthicum v. Archambault, 379 Mass. 381, 388-389 (1979). We have reviewed the rates requested, along with the supporting materials, and believe the rates are reasonable. Accordingly, we award a total of $54,902.78 in fees and the full requested $597.20 in costs, for a total of $55,499.98. Of this amount, we apportion two-thirds, or $36,996.29, to the equity appeal, and one-third, or $18,503.69, to the criminal contempt appeals. Any proceeding to enforce this order shall be commenced in the Middlesex Probate and Family Court. (Green, Massing, Sacks, JJ.) [3] *Notice/Attest/Gibson, J./Christopher, J. Footnotes 1.
