    01/12/2010 #16 ORDER ( By the Court): The parties are directed to address, in writing, the following questions within twenty days of the date of this order: (1) Which State and municipal departments, Federal departments, educational and charitable corporations, and institutions has the commissioner of probation determined, pursuant to G. L. c. 276, sec. 100, may have access to probation records? (2) If it is necessary to state a purpose when requesting copies of such records or when requesting information contained in such records, what are the permitted purposes? (3) Has the commissioner issued any written standards, policies, rules, regulations, or guidelines addressing the information that may be disclosed upon such requests, or to whom such disclosures may be made? If the answer to this question is yes, please provide a complete copy or copies. (4) If a criminal record has been "sealed" pursuant to G.L.c. 276, sec. 100C, what information does the commissioner provide upon receiving such requests? (5) Who may obtain copies of such "sealed" records? (6) What, if any, are the differences between records of the commissioner and CORI records, with respect to the information they contain? (7) Are probation officers employed in District and Superior Courts authorized to disclose information contained in their files, or provide copies of any portions of their files, to persons other than judges in their respective courts? If yes, to whom are they authorized to make such disclosures, and by what authority? Notice to counsel.

    03/25/2010 #19 RESCRIPT (Full Opinion): The portion of the expungement order that is directed to the commissioner is reversed. This case is remanded to the Boston Municipal Court so that the judge may entertain a motion to seal Boe's record in comformity with G.L. c. 276, s 100C. (By the Court) Reasons as on file.
