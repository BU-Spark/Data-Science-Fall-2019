    12/30/2016 #6 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER: On May 18, 2015, the plaintiffs filed a products liability action in Superior Court against both defendants on behalf of their child, Boone Thibeault; included were two counts of loss of consortium for the parents against defendant Nautic Global Group, Inc. (Nautic Global).[4] The complaint alleged that on July 31, 2011, their one year old child was injured while using a ladder supplied by the defendants. Nautic Global filed a motion to dismiss the loss of consortium claims claiming they were barred by a three-year statute of limitations. A Superior Court judge ruled those claims were untimely but ordered that the child's "claims . . . may proceed." The plaintiffs noticed an appeal.[5] A review of the trial court docket reveals that the parties did not move for entry of a separate judgment as to the plaintiffs' claims. See Mass.R.Civ.P. 54(b), 365 Mass. 820 (1974) ("the court may direct the entry of a final judgment as to one or more but fewer than all of the claims or parties only upon an express determination that there is no just reason for delay and upon an express direction for the entry of judgment"). The judge made none of the findings required, see Long v. Wickett, 50 Mass. App. Ct. 380, 387-403 (2000), and no such judgment entered. See note 5, supra. A finding of no just reason for delay requires a searching inquiry ordinarily entrusted to a judge's sound discretion. Long, supra at 386. Absent such inquiry and findings, we cannot say the appeal is properly before us: among other reasons, the plaintiffs' claims appear to be based on essentially the same operative facts as the child's, the record evidences no hardship or injustice recommending immediate review, and the interlocutory order dismissing the plaintiffs' claims remains fully open to appellate review upon entry of a final judgment. See Harrison v. Roncone, 447 Mass. 1001, 1001-1002 (2006); Quincy Mut. Fire Ins. Co. v. Western Sur. Co., 37 Mass. App. Ct. 580, 580-581 (1994). See also Pollack v. Kelly, 372 Mass. 469, 470-472 (1977). Appeal dismissed. (Meade, Henry, Lemire, JJ.)[6] *Notice/Attest Footnotes 1. Individually and as next friend of Boone Thibeault. 2.
