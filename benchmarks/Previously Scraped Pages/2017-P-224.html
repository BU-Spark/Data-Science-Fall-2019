    02/21/2018 #20 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER The Boston Retirement Board commenced this action in the Superior Court seeking judicial review pursuant to G. L. c. 30A, § 14, of a decision by the Contributory Retirement Appeal Board (CRAB), which adopted the determination by an administrative magistrate of the Division of Administrative Law Appeals (DALA) that Dennis Ferreira was entitled to accidental disability retirement benefits. On the parties' cross motions for judgment on the pleadings, the Superior Court judge vacated CRAB's decision and remanded the matter to CRAB for further proceedings. The remand order required that CRAB conduct an evidentiary hearing concerning whether Ferreira was at work at the time that he suffered a myocardial infarction. The judge concluded that the finding on that point by the magistrate was not supported by substantial evidence, in light of the conflicting evidence presented in the record.[2] Ferreira appeals. "It is well established that, in an action seeking judicial review of an administrative agency's decision, no appeal lies from a decision of the trial court remanding the matter to the agency for further proceedings where 'the administrative tribunal has choices to make about the result, in nuance and fundamental conclusion.'" Lankheim v. Board of Registration in Nursing, 454 Mass. 1013, 1014 (2009), quoting from Federman v. Board of Appeals of Marblehead, 35 Mass. App. Ct. 727, 729 (1994). The remand order at issue here contemplates further proceedings before CRAB, including the taking of additional evidence. Accordingly, this interlocutory appeal is not properly before us and must be dismissed.[3] See, e.g., Kelley v. Boston Fire Dept., 86 Mass. App. Ct. 913, 914 (2014) (appeal from Superior Court decision remanding matter to Civil Service Commission for new evidentiary hearing dismissed as interlocutory). Appeal dismissed. (Rubin, Sacks & Wendlandt, JJ.[4]). *Notice/attest/Brieger, J. Footnotes [1] Dennis Ferreira. [2] The remand order noted a discrepancy between Ferreira's account and those contained in medical records of Ferreira's treatment providers concerning whether Ferreira was at work at the time of the myocardial infarction, as well as inconsistencies in Ferreira's testimony. The remand order required consideration of any other evidence placing, or failing to place, Ferreira at work at that time, including payroll records and sign-in sheets. [3] In dismissing this appeal as premature, we do not decide whether the record was insufficient to support CRAB's finding that Ferreira was at work at the time he suffered the myocardial infarction. See Flint v. Commissioner of Pub. Welfare, 412 Mass. 416, 420 (1992) (agency's decision entitled to "highly deferential" review as to facts and reasonable inferences). See also Southern Worcester County Regional Vocational Sch. Dist. v. Labor Relations Commn., 386 Mass. 414, 420 (1982) ("A court may not displace [the agency's] choice between two fairly conflicting views . . ."). [4] The panelists are listed in order of seniority.
