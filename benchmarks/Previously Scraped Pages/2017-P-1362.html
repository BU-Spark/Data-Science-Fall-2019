    12/07/2017   RE#5: This motion is governed by Mass. R. A. P. 6(a). The defendant filed a motion to stay, but the lower court judge declined to rule on it substantively. Therefore, as the stay must ordinarily be sought in the first instance in the lower court, the motion is denied without prejudice to renewal in the Superior Court. It is incumbent on the lower court judge to consider the motion on the merits. If the motion in the Superior Court is denied, the defendant may renew its motion to this Court, but shall comply with the Appeals Court standing order governing motions to stay pursuant to rule 6 and shall address the factors enumerated in Doe v. Superintendent of Schs. of Weston, 461 Mass. 159, 164 (2011). Further I remind the defendant that its motion to the single justice is limited to the grounds for a stay advanced in the motion to the lower court. (Neyman, J.). *Notice/Attest/Wilson, J.
