    01/26/2016 #4 ORDER: The plaintiff, Nancy Chadwick, has petitioned the Single Justice pursuant to G. L. c. 231, § 118, for relief from an order by a judge of the Superior Court requiring her to respond to discovery propounded by the defendants in the underlying suit. At issue is Chadwick's claim that communications between her and her union representatives should be protected from discovery because they are privileged. Her claim presents an issue of first impression in the Commonwealth.[1] Privileges represent an exception to the general duty to provide relevant testimony. Commonwealth v. Oliveira, 438 Mass. 325, 330 (2002), and cases cited therein. Mass. G. Evid. § 500-527 (2015). They are often, but not exclusively, created by statute.[2] Their purpose is to promote and defend policy goals held to outweigh the general duty to testify. "Although [the Supreme Judicial Court] has the power to create privileges, it is a power that we have exercised sparingly, and only to the very limited extent that permitting a refusal to testify or excluding relevant evidence has a public good transcending the normally predominant principle of utilizing all rational means for ascertaining truth." Babets v. Secretary of Human Servs., 403 Mass. 230, 234 (1988) citing Three Juveniles v. Commonwealth, 390 Mass. 357, 359-60 (1983) (quotation omitted). The union member-union representative privilege asserted by Chadwick has, as stated, been neither promulgated by the legislature nor adopted by common law in Massachusetts.[3] While some states have recognized the privilege,[4] most have not done so. The judge declined to create a previously unrecognized privilege in the course of discovery, and I consider that to have been the appropriate ruling. It was neither clear error nor an abuse of discretion. Aspinall v. Philip Morris Cos., Inc., 442 Mass. 381, 390 (2004). The amicus and the parties' submissions in the Superior Court and to the Single Justice have cited a wide range of authority and comprehensively briefed the policy issues. Accordingly, the judge's order requiring production is stayed for seven days from the date of this order to allow the plaintiff an opportunity to seek further review should she wish to do so. The petition is otherwise denied. (Grainger, J.). Notice/attest/Yessayan, J.     02/01/2016 #5 Request for report to full bench of the court pursuant to G.L. c. 231, §188 filed by Nancy Chadwick ^
    02/05/2016   ORDER: This matter first came before this Court pursuant to G.L. c. 231, § 118 (1st para.) on petitioner-plaintiff's request for interlocutory review of a discovery order entered by the Plymouth Superior Court (Yessan, J.) on December 17, 2015. The order required the plaintiff to respond to discovery that the plaintiff claims is privileged and therefore protected. The discovery at issue concerns communications between the plaintiff and her union representatives. On January 26, 2016, the single justice issued an order denying the petition for interlocutory review, and staying the underlying matter for seven days to allow the plaintiff the opportunity to seek further review. The matter is again before the single justice on plaintiff's request for leave to take an interlocutory appeal from the December 17, 2015 order of the trial court. As stated in the single justice's January 26, 2016 order disposing of the petition, the plaintiff's claim presents an issue of first impression in the Commonwealth. Because the question is likely to recur in future cases, I report the question presented by this case to a panel of this court. The temporary stay granted on January 26, 2016, is to remain in effect until further order of this court. The plaintiff's appeal is to proceed on an expedited basis in accordance with the following procedures. The plaintiff shall be treated as the appellant and the defendants as the appellees. The matter is hereby entered on the docket as 16-P-164, without payment of any additional fee or further assembly of the record. The trial court is to transmit an updated docket entry sheet to this court forthwith. The appellant shall file her brief and appendix on or before March 11, 2016, and the defendants-appellees' brief shall be filed on or before April 11, 2016. No party shall seek an enlargement of time to file a brief, except for extraordinary circumstances. The appeal shall be scheduled for argument in May, 2016. (Grainger, J.). *Notice/Attest/Yessayan, J.
