Where the subject of a grand jury's investigation is an adult, whether the court should adopt a rule similar to the rule adopted for juveniles in Commonwealth v. Walczak, 463 Mass. 808 (2012) (requiring that "where the Commonwealth seeks to indict a juvenile for murder and where there is substantial evidence of mitigating circumstances or defenses [other than lack of criminal responsibility] presented to the grand jury, the prosecutor shall instruct the grand jury on the elements of murder and on the significance of the mitigating circumstances and defenses"); whether any such rule would apply to a defendant who appeals from convictions following a jury trial, on indictments that issued prior to the decision in Walczak, and who requested grand jury instructions before the decision in Walczak, and moved to dismiss the indictments; whether the jury was properly instructed on the elements of arson, see G. L. c. 266, § 1.
