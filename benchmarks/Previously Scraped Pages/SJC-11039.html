    02/27/2013 #3 ORDER: In September, 2011, the defendant appealed from a judgment of a single justice of this court denying his petition for relief under G. L. c. 211, § 3. The petition sought relief from a harassment prevention order issued by a judge in the Newton District Court. The defendant had also pursued, in May, 2011, an appeal from the harassment prevention order in the Appeals Court; that appeal was pending when the defendant filed his appeal from the denial of his G. L. c. 211, § 3, petition in this court. In January, 2012, this court decided O'Brien v. Borowski, 461 Mass. 415 (2012), in which we concluded that, in the future, appeals from harassment prevention orders issued pursuant to G. L. c. 258E should proceed in the Appeals Court. We held the defendants G. L. c. 211, § 3, appeal pending the outcome of the defendant's appeal in the Appeals Court. The Appeals Court thereafter considered the appeal and dismissed it as moot. "We now invite the parties to submit supplemental briefing on the issue whether an appeal from a harassment prevention order that expires while the appeal is pending must be dismissed as moot, and, if so, whether the defendant's appeal to the Appeals Court in this case constituted an adequate alternative remedy for purposes of her G. L. c. 211, § 3, petition. "This case will be paired for argument with Seney v. Morhy, SJC No. FAR-21213, in which the court has today granted the defendant's application for further appellate review.
