    12/12/2016 #7 ORDER: Deborah Druley, a beneficiary of the estate of Robert B. Roderick, appeals from a Probate and Family Court order dated June 18, 2015, authorizing and directing the estate's personal representative to convey certain real property to another beneficiary at a specified price. Although appealable under G. L. c. 215, § 9, the order is not a final judgment, and an appeal from such an order lies dormant until a final judgment is entered. See Borman v. Borman, 378 Mass. 775, 778-780 (1979); Beit v. Probate & Family Court Dept., 385 Mass. 854, 857 (1982); Winternitz v. Winternitz, 19 Mass. App. Ct. 228, 230 n.1 (1985)[1]. The docket sheet included in Druley's record appendix indicates that there are ongoing proceedings in this matter in the trial court, and we take judicial notice of the current docket sheet indicating that a motion to continue or reschedule a trial was allowed on November 2, 2016. Accordingly, appellate proceedings are hereby stayed, and Druley is ordered to show cause, in writing, on or before December 23, 2016, why jurisdiction over this appeal is proper, or in the alternative to move for an order vacating the notice of assembly of the record and entry of the appeal as premature. See Mancuso v. Mancuso, 10 Mass. App. Ct. 395, 401 (1980); Halperson v. Halperson, 65 Mass. App. Ct. 909, 909-910 (2006). See also Gorod v. Tabachnick, 428 Mass. 1001, 1002 (1998). The appellees may, if they wish, file a reply within 14 days thereafter. (Cypher, Massing & Sacks, JJ.). [2] *Notice/Attest Footnotes 1. "Where, as in this case, interlocutory appeals claimed in a proceeding in a Probate Court come before us prematurely for review, . . . [the appellant] is not at fault. . . . In such a case the procedural error is the issuance of the notice of assembly of the record, which activates the deadline for entry and all other significant procedural steps." Mancuso v. Mancuso, 10 Mass. App. Ct. 395, 402 (1990). 2. The panelists are listed in order of seniority.
