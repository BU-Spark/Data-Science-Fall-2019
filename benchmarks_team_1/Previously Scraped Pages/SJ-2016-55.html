    05/11/2016 #11 Memorandum of Decision: ... "The petitioner's claims are neither new nor substantial within the meaning of G. L. c. 278, ' 33E. The issues the petitioner raises in this petition relative to the asserted ineffective assistance of his trial counsel, the asserted inability to choose his counsel, and the asserted potential conflict of interest, are the same as those raised in his previous gatekeeper petition, which were also raised in his April, 2012 motion for a new trial. The issue related to a purported inadequate review by the full court, during his direct appeal, of the entire trial transcript, was addressed by the Commonwealth during the proceedings on the motion for a new trial. The issue could have been, but was not, raised in the petitioner's direct appeal. See Commonwealth v. Gunter, supra. Nor does it suggest that justice may not have been done. The court's decision states explicitly that it conducted a plenary review of the entire trial record and found no reason to grant relief under G. L. c. 278, ' 33E, noting that "the evidence of the [petitioner's] guilt was so overwhelming" that certain evidentiary errors did not prejudice the petitioner and did not require a new trial. See Commonwealth v. Zagranski, supra at 278, 283-284. 3. Conclusion. An order shall enter denying the petitioner's petition filed under G. L. c. 211, ' 3." (Lenk, J.)
    05/11/2016 #12 JUDGMENT: ... "it is ORDERED that the petitioner's petition pursuant to G. L. c. 211, § 3, shall be, and hereby is, DENIED." (Lenk, J.)
