    09/27/2017   RE#1: Defendants High Pines, LLC and Frederick W. Tonsberg have filed a petition pursuant to G.L. c. 231, s. 118 (first para) seeking review of the Plymouth Superior Court's (Gildea, J.) order for a preliminary injunction issued to Plaintiffs High Pines, Corp. and Indian Pond Country Club, Inc. The order entered on the Superior Court docket on August 21, 2017, and the petition was filed in the Appeals Court on September 26, 2017. General Laws c. 231, s. 118 (first par.) provides that a petition for relief may be filed "within thirty days of the entry of such order." The thirty-day limit is a statutory limit that the single justice is without authority to enlarge. Note that Mass.R.A.P. 14(b) does not apply to a time limit established by statute. Accordingly, if the petition is filed late, the single justice has no jurisdiction to act on the petition. Cf. Friedman v. Board of Regis. in Medicine, 414 Mass. 663, 665 (1993); Nissan Motor Corp. v. Commissioner of Revenue, 407 Mass. 153, 157 (1990). "Entry" is the date the order or action is entered on the docket of the trial court. See Standard Register Co. v. Bolton-Emerson, Inc., 35 Mass. App. Ct. 570, 571-572 (1993); Abbott v. John Hancock Mut. Life Ins. Co., 18 Mass. App. Ct. 508, 511 n.6 (1984). Defendants' claim that the order is dated August 28, 2017, appears to be based on the date of notice which is not controlling. Accordingly, the petition is untimely as to the order of August 21, 2017, and the petition is dismissed. (Lemire, J). Notice/attest/Gildea, J.

    10/13/2017 #6 ORDER: High Pines, LLC and Frederick Tonsberg have petitioned pursuant to M.G.L. c. 231, sec. 118 (first para.) for interlocutory reversal of the trial judge's allowance of partial summary judgment that enjoined High Pines, LLC and Frederick Tonsberg from using a certain way and easement to access properties formerly owned by William J. O'Brien and Arrowhead Farms Realty Trust for any purpose. Alternatively, the petition requests leave to seek review by a full panel of the Appeals Court. Upon consideration of all petition materials, the single justice DENIES appellants' request. I see no reason to disturb the trial judge's ruling, as it has not been made to appear that the judge abused his discretion or committed clear error of law. See generally, Jet-Line Services, Inc. v. Board of Selectmen of Stoughton, 25 Mass App. Ct. 645, 646 (1988). Further, plenary consideration ordinarily is reserved for review of issues following entry of judgment, either on all issues or on certification of severable claims under Mass. R. Civ. P. 54(b). The petition does not present any issue of such significance as would warrant accelerated consideration by a panel. (Lemire, J.). Notice/attest/Gildea, J.
