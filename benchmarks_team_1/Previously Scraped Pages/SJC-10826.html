    09/11/2014 #28 ORDER: The issuance of the rescript to the trial court is hereby STAYED until further order of the court. The court directs the parties to submit supplemental memoranda setting forth their views as to how, if at all, the holding in Maryland v. Shatzer, 559 U.S. 98 (2010), applies to this case. The memoranda shall be filed within fourteen days of this order and shall not exceed fifteen pages in length. Without limiting the issues to be addressed in the supplemental memoranda, the court directs the parties, at a minimum, to answer two questions: (1) Does the holding in Maryland v. Shatzer apply retroactively? (2) If the opinion does apply retroactively, and if the July 7, 2006 pre-arrest interview of the defendant was in violation of violation of Edwards v. Arizona, 451 U.S. 477 (1981), did the Commonwealth prove beyond a reasonable doubt that the defendant, during the booking following her arrest, "'initiate[d] further communication, exchanges, or conversations with the police,' and thereby waived h[er] right to counsel"? Commonwealth v. Hoyt, 461 Mass. 143, 151 (2011), quoting Edwards, 451 U.S. at 485. Nothing in this order limits er limits the parties' ability to file petitions for rehearing in the usual course, raising any additional points they wish to make. Petitions for rehearing must be filed, in accordance with Mass. R. A. P. 27, on or before September 16, 2014. The supplemental memoranda required by this order shall be filed in addition to any petitions for rehearing. After the court has received and considered the parties' submissions, both the supplemental memoranda and any petitions for rehearing, it will decide whether any change in the opinion is necessary.
