    05/12/2017 #1 Petition pursuant to G.L. c. 231, § 118 with attachments, filed by Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation, Ocwen Loan Servicing, LLC.
    05/12/2017 #2 Motion for enlargement of time to file Petition for Interlocutory Review, filed by Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation, Ocwen Loan Servicing, LLC.
    05/18/2017   ORDER: The plaintiffs seek review of an order entered in the Superior Court on March 2, 2017. The petition was filed on May 12, 2017, more than thirty days following entry of the order. The thirty-day time limit is established by G. L. c. 231, § 118, ¶ 1; it is jurisdictional and may not be enlarged. The filing of a motion for reconsideration in the lower court does not enlarge or create a new thirty-day time period. McGrath v. McGrath, 65 Mass. App. Ct. 670, 671 (2006). Moreover, an order denying a motion for reconsideration, unless a substantially new or newly-supported motion, does not provide a separate basis for relief under G. L. c. 231, § 118, ¶ 1. Id. at 672. See also Selby Associates v. Boston Redevelopment Auth., 27 Mass. App. Ct. 1188, 1189-1190 (1989); Manousos v. Sarkis, 382 Mass. 317, 322-323 (1981). Review of the two motions presented to the lower court reveals no substantial difference between the relief sought or the grounds relied upon. The plaintiffs' petition identifies no respect in which their motion for reconsideration presented matters for determination by the motion judge that were substantially different from those presented in their original motion; indeed their petition requests relief from both the denial of their original motion and from the denial of their motion for reconsideration. Accordingly, insofar as the petition seeks review of the original order, it is untimely. Insofar as it seeks review of the motion for reconsideration, I discern no abuse of discretion on the part of the lower court judge. All relief requested in the petition is denied. (Green, J.). Notice/attest/Inge, J.
