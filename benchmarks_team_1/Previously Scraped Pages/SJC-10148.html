    05/02/2008 #3 ORDER: Appeal allowed to proceed. "The Commonwealth appeals from a judgment of a single justice of this court denying its petition for relief pursuant to GLc211, s3. The Commonwealth had sought relief from an order of the Superior Court directing the Commonwealth in a pending criminal case to disclose to the defendant -- charged with various drug offenses and planning to pursue an entrapment defense -- the identities of any government informants used by the Commonwealth and any benefits provided in return for the informants' assistance. Specifically, the Commonwealth was ordered to disclose whether a person known to the defendant (Christin Gross) was a government agent or acting at the direction of the government, see Comm. v. Madigan, 449 Mass. 702 (2007), and to disclose the identities of any other government informants. In support of the defendant's motion, she stated in an affidavit, in pertinent part: 'Christin Gross, an acquaintance of mine, continually told me she had someone she wanted me to meet named Ryan. I told her I had health issues and didn't want to get involved with anybody at that time. She called me again and urged me to reconsider meeting Ryan. I finally agreed to do so. At the first meeting on 12/2/2006, Ryan requested a pain killer to ease his back pain. Christin said she didn't have any and asked if I would give him one, which I did. I handed it to Christin and she gave it to Ryan.' In its GLc211, s3 petition, the Commownealth argued that the defendant's affidavit was insufficient to show 'some evidence of indicement.' Comm. v. Miller, 361 Mass. 644, 651 (1972). See also Comm. v. Tracey, 416 Mass. 528, 536 (1993)(conduct possessing indicia of inducement include 'aggressive persuasion, coercive encouragement, lengthy negotiations, pleading or arguing with the defendant, repeated or persistent solicitation, persuasion, importuning, or playing on sympathy or other emotion'). The single justice allowed the petition in part and denied it in part. He concluded, in relevant respects: 'While I agree with the Commonwealth that the defendant's affidavit seems particularly weak on the principal question (about which she should know the most) of whether this named person repeatedly encouraged her to meet with the undercover officer for romantic purposes or for the purpose of selling him drugs, I am not inclined to substitute my judgment for that of the judge on this point. However, to the extent that the motion allowed by the judge includes language that requires the disclosure of information about anyone other than this named person, it should have been denied. In sum, the judge's order that the Commonwealth disclose to the defendant whether this named person was acting as an agent of or at the direction of the Commonwealth, and if so, what if any benefits were provided in return for this assistance is affirmed. Disclosures with respect to any other unidentified person is, based on the present record, denied.' The case is now before this court pursuant to SJC Rule 2:21.... The Commonwealth appeals from that portion of the Superior Court order that the single justice affirmed. Because the Superior Court order that the Commonwealth challenges is interlocutory, the Commonwealth must 'set forth the reasons why review of the trial court decision cannot adequately be obtained on appeal from any final adverse judgment in the trial court or by other available means.' Rule 2:21(2). We agree with the Commonwealth that it cannot adequately obtain appellate review of the order at issue other than pursuant to GLc211, s3. Without addressing the underlying merits of the Commonwealth's appeal, we conclude the Commonwealth lacks an adequate alternative remedy. Accordingly, the Commonwealth may proceed with its appeal in the regular course." By the Court. (Notice to counsel.)
