    01/05/2018 #23 Reservation and Report as on file. (Gants, C.J.)..."Because of the importance of the issues in this case, I hereby reserve and report to the full court so much of the case as involves the board's motion to dismiss. The board, as the moving party, is designated as the appellant. I will retain the remainder of the case for the time being, and if, after the full court's decision, any claim alleged in the complaint survives the board's motion to dismiss, I will then decide on an appropriate course for resolving the complaint on the merits. Among the issues presented in the Noe appeal, the board challenges the judge's conclusion that the board "committed an error of law and violated [the plaintiff's] rights under both the Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution and art. 12 of the Massachusetts Declaration of Rights, by placing the burden on [the plaintiff] to prove at his reclassification hearing in February, 2014, that his risk of re-offense and the degree of dangerousness he poses to the public had decreased since his original classification in 2007." The plaintiff in that case did not contest the quantum of proof at a classification hearing, which, in February, 2014, was a preponderance of the evidence. In 2016, however, the board changed the quantum of proof at a reclassification hearing to clear and convincing evidence. See 803 Code Mass. Regs. §1.31 (2) (c) (2016). See also 803 Code Mass. Regs. § 1.30 (1) & (2) (b) (2016) (same with respect to motions to terminate registration obligation). The plaintiffs here challenge the constitutionality of that quantum of proof. Therefore, to ensure that this important legal issue regarding the quantum of proof is reached, along with the issue in the Noe case regarding who bears the burden of proof, I am also reporting to the full court the following question of law that is presented by this case: "What is the quantum of proof required at a hearing where a sex offender requests reclassification or termination of his or her status as a registered sex offender?" The appeal shall be entered in the full court forthwith. The record shall consist of all the papers that are before me pertinent to the board's motion to dismiss. The matter shall be heard at the court's April, 2018, sitting. The board's brief as appellant will be due on February 14, 2018; the plaintiffs' and intervener's briefs will be due on March 16, 2018; and any reply brief will be due on March 28, 2018. No extensions of time for briefing should be anticipated. In all other respects, this matter shall proceed in conformance with the Massachusetts Rules of Appellate Procedure. Because of the connection between this case and the board's appeal in the Noe case, I further order that the board's appeal in the Noe case also be heard at the April sitting. "
