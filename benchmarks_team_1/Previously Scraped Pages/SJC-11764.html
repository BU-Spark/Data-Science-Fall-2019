    12/19/2014 #13 CLERK'S LETTER to all counsel of record: As a general practice, Justice Lenk recuses herself from cases in which the Committee for Public Counsel Services is a party see, e.g., Edwards, petitioner, 464 Mass. 454 (2013) because her spouse, attorney Debra S. Krupp, is employed at CPCS. Justice Lenk does not necessarily recuse herself from every case in which CPCS attorneys represent a client, however, and she ordinarily does not recuse herself in cases in which CPCS files an amicus brief. CPCS has moved to intervene in this case, and the single justice reserved and reported that motion to the full court as part of her reservation and report of the entire case. The full court will therefore be required to act on the motion, and, if it is allowed, then to consider on the merits the claims asserted by CPCS. It appears that CPCS seeks to intervene primarily in order to assert the rights of its present and future clients in the subject matter, and not to assert any rights and interests of CPCS as an organization per se. Justice Lenk is informed that attorney Krupp has had no involvement in this case, either directly or indirectly, and that she has no supervisory responsibility over the attorneys representing CPCS. Additionally, Justice Lenk has satisfied herself that she has no preconceived views on the results of this case and that she can and will be impartial in judging the matter. The parties are asked to consider whether they believe there is any reason why Justice Lenk should recuse herself in this case, and to let the court know in writing, on or before December 29, 2014, whether they agree, or have any objection, to Justice Lenk participating on the quorum. Justice Lenk will make a final decision regarding recusal after considering the parties' responses. The Clerk shall enter this statement, along with the responses received by the parties, on the docket for the case.
