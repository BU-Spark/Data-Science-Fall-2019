    09/12/2017   RE#7: The motion to stay is allowed for reasons unrelated to those set forth in the motion. The parties are to note that the Superior Court denied Plaintiff Stephen Ranieri's motion to file a late notice of appeal on 9/8/17. Ranieri has no similar motion pending before the Appeals Court. Accordingly, Defendant Derek Glacken and Third-Party Defendant William Paulsen are the only parties to have entered their respective appeal and cross-appeal. The parties are to note that in all appeals before the court each party may only file either a blue appellant brief or red appellee brief, and to the extent that an appeal involves a consolidated action in which a party is both an appellant as to one aspect of the underlying decision and an appellee as to another, the rules involving cross-appeals apply. See MRAP 16(i). Appellate proceedings are stayed to 10/2/17. The parties are to confer during that time and propose a briefing schedule that contemplates which parties will be filing a blue brief and appendix and which parties will be filing a red brief. The proposed briefing schedule may be staggered with one blue brief becoming due before another, but no appealing party should anticipate filing more than one principal brief of 50 pages or less and one reply brief of 20 pages or less. The proposed briefing schedule due on or before 10/2/17. *Notice.
