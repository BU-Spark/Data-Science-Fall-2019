    11/29/2017 #4 ORDER: On June 20, 2017, the Maine Supreme Judicial Court issued an order suspending Robert M.A. Nadeau (respondent) from the practice of law for two years, commencing August 31, 2017. The Maine Supreme Judicial Court determined that the respondent, while acting as a judge or a judge-elect in Maine, violated the 1993 Maine Code of Judicial Conduct, Canons 2 (A), 2 (B), and 3 (C)(4), and the 2015 Maine Code of Judicial Conduct, Canon 4.2 (C)(1). Bar counsel filed a petition for reciprocal discipline in Massachusetts, pursuant to S.J.C. Rule 4:01, § 16, as appearing in 425 Mass. 1319 (1997). A single justice of this court issued an order directing the respondent to inform the court why "the imposition of the identical discipline imposed in" Maine would be unwarranted, and the respondent filed a response. See S.J.C. Rule 4:01, § 16. After a hearing, the single justice ordered the respondent suspended from the practice of law in Massachusetts for eighteen months. The respondent now appeals from the single justice's order of term suspension. Following the procedure established by this court for expediting appeals in bar discipline cases, the respondent has filed a preliminary memorandum and record appendix. See S.J.C. Rule 2:23, as appearing in 471 Mass. 1303 (2015). The determination whether reciprocal discipline is warranted depends, among other things, on whether the misconduct established in the Maine proceedings "in practical application and effect" constitutes a violation of one or more of the Massachusetts Rules of Professional Conduct. See Matter of Dwyer-Jones, 470 Mass. 582, 586 (2015) (while "rules and procedures of other jurisdictions may not align perfectly with ours, . . . if they are 'equivalent' in practical application and effect, orders and judgments entered elsewhere serve as the basis for reciprocal treatment here"). Before deciding this appeal, and determining whether reciprocal discipline properly was imposed, it would be helpful to the court to have clarification as to the basis for the discipline. We therefore remand the matter to the single justice so that she can indicate expressly whether the respondent's conduct violated any of the Massachusetts Rules of Professional Conduct by a preponderance of the evidence and, if so, identify which rule or rules; if the respondent's conduct did not violate a specific rule, whether reciprocal discipline nonetheless is warranted and why; and the basis for her determination that an eighteen-month suspension is the appropriate sanction. Further proceedings in this appeal shall be stayed pending an explanatory memorandum of decision from the single justice, which shall be entered on the docket of the bar disciplinary proceeding in the county court and then transmitted to this court. After the single justice's memorandum is entered, either party may request leave to return to the county court to seek reconsideration, if appropriate, from the single justice. By the Court
    01/05/2018 #5 Copy of a single justice's Memorandum of Decision (docket no. BD-2017-077) received. "I therefore concluded that a term of suspension of eighteen months rather than two years was appropriate. Accordingly, an order entered suspending the respondent from the practice of law for eighteen months." (Budd, J.).
    01/11/2018 #6 ORDER: The single justice's explanatory memorandum of decision having been received, the stay of further proceedings in this appeal entered on November 29, 2017, is vacated. The respondent may file a supplement to the preliminary memorandum he previously filed addressing the single justice's memorandum of decision. The supplement may not exceed ten pages, and must be filed within fourteen days of this order. Bar counsel may file a memorandum pursuant to S.J.C. Rule 2:23 (b), within fourteen days thereafter. By the Court.
    01/29/2018 #7 STIPULATION OF DISMISSAL, filed by the parties. (Per the within, pursuant to Mass. R.A.P. 29(b), appeal is dismissed.)
