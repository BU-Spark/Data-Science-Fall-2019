    11/29/2012 #22 ORDER (By the Court): In Denver Street LLC v. Saugus, 462 Mass. 651, (2012), we vacated the lower court judgment for the plaintiff developers and ordered judgment to enter for the town. In its brief to this court, the town did not include a request for costs as required by Fabre v. Walton, 441 Mass. 9, 10-11 (2004). In October, 2012, the town filed a motion pursuant to Mass. R. A. P. 26 (a) & (c), in the Superior Court, seeking $4,678.20, representing the cost of reproducing appellate briefs, record appendices and trial transcripts. The judge denied the motion without prejudice, stating that under the plain language of rule 26 (a), where a judgment is vacated on appeal, the award of costs may only be "directed by the appellate court." The town then filed a motion in this court requesting an order directing the Superior Court to grant appellate costs. The town argues that, even though this court's decision stated that the judgments should be vacated, "the effect of this Court's decision was a reversal of the judgments entered below." The town further asserts, in essence, that the language of rule 26 governing reversal of judgments applies, therefore no appellate court order is necessary. We agree with the judge that, under the plain language of rule 26 (a), where, as here, a judgment was vacated, an order by the appellate court is required. Because the town failed to include a request for costs in its brief, the request is waived. Beal Bank, SSB v. Eurich, 448 Mass. 9, 12 (2006) (failure to request fees and costs in brief constitutes waiver); Fabre v. Walton, supra. For the reasons herein stated, it is hereby ORDERED that the town's motion for an order for costs is DENIED. (Notice)
