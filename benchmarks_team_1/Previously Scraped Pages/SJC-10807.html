    04/20/2011 #14 ORDER on Motion of Plaintiff-Appellant PCG Trading, LLC, to Stay Proceedings Pending Appeal (By the Court): PCG Trading, LLC (PCG), has moved to stay further proceedings in the underlying action during the pendency of PCG's appeal in this court; PCG brings this motion after a judge in the Superior Court denied a similar motion filed by PCG filed in that court. PCG claims that insofar as it is appealing the denial of its motion to allow its counsel of choice, an attorney licensed in New York State, to appear pro hac vice, it would be unjust to require PCG to proceed with the underlying litigation and completion of discovery, expert witness preparation and dispositive motion practice in particular while its appeal is pending. The defendants oppose the motion, arguing that PCG filed this action in July, 2009, in the Superior Court; there have already been long delays in the conduct of this case; PCG has previously taken the position that it would not seek a stay in order to appeal the pro hac vice issue; in reliance on that position, depositions have been scheduled; there is a trial date scheduled for January, 2012; and it would be unjust to delay the trial beyond that date. After consideration, the motion to stay is denied. There appears to be no reason that PCG's New York attorney may not work with and provide material guidance and assistance to local counsel in completing discovery (including attending depositions and consulting with local counsel during them), and in preparation of the case on behalf of PCG.(fn) This court's decision is likely to be forthcoming well in advance of the trial date in January of 2012, and it is therefore unlikely that PCG's appeal will be rendered moot if the requested stay is not granted.(fn:With respect to dispositive motion practice, it appears there is a motion for partial summary judgment scheduled to be argued on May 17, 2011. In light of the fact that PCG's appeal in this court was argued on April 7, 2011, and the trial is scheduled for January of 2012, it may be possible to delay the argument of that motion until this court's decision is issued. PCG is free to request the Superior Court judge to delay the argument on that pending motion to a somewhat later date.).
    07/29/2011 #15 RESCRIPT (Full Opinion): The order denying the motion to admit Robert L. Garner pro hac vice is reversed, and the case is remanded to the Superior Court for further proceedings consistent with this opinion. (By the Court) Reasons as on file. Notice sent.
