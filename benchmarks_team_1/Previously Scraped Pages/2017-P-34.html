    02/21/2018 #17 ORDER Pursuant to Mass.R.Crim.P. 15(d), as appearing in 422 Mass. 1501 (1996), the defendant has filed a petition for attorney's fees totaling $13,190. The Commonwealth has filed an opposition. For the reasons that follow, the petition is allowed in the amount of $9,272.00. Two private attorneys, Merritt Schnipper and John M. Thompson, represented the defendant in connection with the Commonwealth's interlocutory appeal. The defendant has requested payment for 54.4 hours of work performed by Schnipper and 7.7 hours of work performed by Thompson. The Commonwealth does not dispute that the rates charged by the attorneys are reasonable,[1] or that the hours claimed were incurred in the service of the defendant. Rather, it argues that the attorneys inappropriately billed for performing administrative tasks and that the number of hours billed is excessive. "What constitutes a reasonable fee is a question that is committed to the sound discretion of the judge." Berman v. Linnane, 434 Mass. 301, 302-303 (2001). In deciding whether a fee is reasonable, we "properly exercise[] independent judgment concerning the request's reasonableness." Stowe v. Bologna, 417 Mass. 199, 204 (1994). "[W]e consider 'the nature of the case and the issues presented, the time and labor required, the amount of damages involved, the result obtained, the experience, reputation and ability of the attorney, the usual price charged for similar services by other attorneys in the same area, and the amount of awards in similar cases.'" Haddad v. Wal-Mart Stores, Inc., 455 Mass. 1024, 1025 (2010), quoting from Linthicum v. Archambault, 379 Mass. 381, 388-389 (1979). We are not obliged to "review and allow or disallow each individual item in the bill, but [may] consider the bill as a whole." Berman v. Linnane, supra at 303. After reviewing the defendant's motion and supporting materials, the briefs filed and the record in this matter, the time expended, counsels' level of expertise and experience, the fees customarily charged for similar work, and "the bill as a whole," ibid., we conclude that the requested award is excessive. The defendant shall be awarded attorney's fees in the amount of $9,272.00, consisting of $7,172.00 for 35.86 hours of work performed by Schnipper and $2,100.00 for 7 hours of work performed by Thompson. Accordingly, it is ordered that, pursuant to Mass.R.Crim.P. 15(d), the Commonwealth pay the defendant $9,272.00 as reasonable attorney's fees related to the Commonwealth's interlocutory appeal. It is further ordered that subject to appropriation, fifty percent of this amount shall be paid from the budget of the Administrative Office of the Trial Court. The defendant has not sought costs, and no amount is awarded in relation to costs. Any proceeding to enforce this order shall be commenced in the Superior Court.
