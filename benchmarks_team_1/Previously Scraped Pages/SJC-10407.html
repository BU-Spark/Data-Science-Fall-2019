    11/22/2010 #20 ORDER: On November 16, 2010, the Commonwealth filed a motion in this case requesting to stay appellate proceedings pending the correction of various alleged omissions in the transcript by the court reporter. The procedural background of the motion to stay is significant. This case is an appeal from a conviction of murder in the first degree. The defendant, Linrose Woodbine, was tried and convicted in October, 2007. His appeal was entered on the docket of this court in 2009. The docket in this court reflects the following: after several motions to extend the filing date for his brief were allowed, the defendant filed his brief on March 15, 2010; after two motions to extend the filing date for its brief, the Commonwealth s brief was due to be filed on September 23, 2010; the Commonwealth has not filed any further motions to extend the filing date, but did not file its brief on September 23, 2010, and has not filed its brief to date; on November 16, 2010, three weeks before the scheduled argument in this case, the Commonwealth, through its appellate counsel Macy Lee, filed the present motion to stay; the motion was not accompanied by any affidavit concerning the factual basis for the motion, or a copy of the transcript volume or volumes that were alleged to need correction; on November 17, 2010, the defendant filed an opposition to the motion to stay, attaching copies of the transcript volume or volumes apparently in question, which on their face appear complete and without omissions. Finally, on November 18, 2010, the Commonwealth filed an affidavit of its appellate counsel that sets forth the factual basis for the Commonwealth s representation that there are omissions in the transcript. The affidavit also explains that appellate counsel had undergone surgery in late October, 2010, that has interfered with her ability to complete the Commonwealth s brief. The Commonwealth still has not filed a motion to extend or enlarge the filing date for its brief. Appellate proceedings in this court are governed by the Massachusetts Rules of Appellate Procedure. Rule 19(d) governs the filing and service of briefs in appeals of convictions of murder in the first degree, and requires the Commonwealth to serve and file its brief within ninety days after service of the defendant s brief. Pursuant to rule 14(b), a party may seek enlargement of time for filing its brief, but the party must do so by written motion. The Commonwealth, as is true of any party to an appeal, is required to comply with those rules. Despite the Commonwealth s repeated failure to follow the requirements of the rules of appellate procedure in this case, in light of the information contained in the late-filed affidavit of the Commonwealth s appellate counsel, the Commonwealth s motion to stay is allowed. It is further ordered that: 1. The appeal in this case is set for argument on February 11, 2011; 2. The Commonwealth is to serve on the defendant and file in this court any corrections to the transcript in this case on or before January 11, 2011; 3. The Commonwealth is to serve and file its brief on or before January 18, 2011; and 4. No further enlargements of time will be permitted in the absence of extraordinary circumstances. By the Court, Clerk Entered: November 22, 2010
