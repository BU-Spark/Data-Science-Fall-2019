    07/21/2016 #18 ORDER: This is the Commonwealth's appeal from an adverse suppression ruling. The Commonwealth was granted leave to appeal by a single justice of this court pursuant to Mass. R. Crim. P. 15 (a) (2). While the appeal has been pending, the defendant pleaded guilty to a lesser offense in the trial court, and the underlying case was resolved on that basis. The Commonwealth now moves pursuant to Mass. R. A. P. 29 (b) to dismiss its appeal as moot. While we agree with the defendant that the issue i.e., the admissibility of so-called ShotSpotter evidence is important and capable of repetition, there is nothing about it that will necessarily evade appellate review in future cases, either in a direct appeal in the event a defendant is convicted based in part on ShotSpotter evidence, or, as here, in an interlocutory appeal from a suppression ruling that has been authorized by a single justice. The only reason the issue evades review in this case is as a consequence of the defendant's change of plea. We will not require the Commonwealth to proceed further with its appeal in these circumstances. Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed as moot. This order shall constitute the rescript for purposes of Mass. R. A. P. 1 and Mass. R. Crim. P. 15 (d). If the defendant is represented by private counsel, he may apply for his costs and reasonable attorney's fees pursuant to rule 15 (d), supported by appropriate documentation, within thirty days of the date of this order. See Commonwealth v. Augustine, 470 Mass. 837, 841 (2015); Commonwealth v. Rosario, 458 Mass. 1003 (2010). By the Court.
