    05/24/2017 #15 RESCRIPT (Full Opinion): Although the important and special role of the drug court in achieving important public policy interests is acknowledged and appreciated, the court is constrained to follow the clear dictates of G. L. c. 211D and S.J.C. Rule 3:10, which vest the Committee for Public Counsel Services with sole and independent authority to assign counsel for indigent defendants. Where a drug court defendant who is indigent is alleged to have violated the terms of probation, the judge must appoint CPCS as counsel. Counsel assigned by CPCS may be removed only for cause after a hearing. (By the Court)
