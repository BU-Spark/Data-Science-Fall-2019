    01/30/2017 #5 ORDER: After hearing and review of the judge's findings and papers filed below, the order of the trial court, entered pursuant to G.L. c. 261, §27D, is affirmed. General Laws c. 261, §27C provides that the judge "shall not deny any request with respect to extra fees and costs if it finds the document, service or object is reasonably necessary to assure the applicant as effective a prosecution or defense as he would have if he were financially able to pay." This standard has been determined to be "essentially one of reasonableness, and looks to whether a defendant who was able to pay and was paying the expenses himself, would consider the 'document, service or object' sufficiently important that he would choose to obtain it in preparation for his trial. The test is not whether a particular item or service would be acquired by a defendant who had unlimited resources, nor is it whether the item might conceivably contribute some assistance to the defense or prosecution by the indigent person." Commonwealth v. Lockley, 381 Mass. 156, 160 (1980). Although she initially allowed the defendant's motion for funds for two experts in addition to his Qualified Examiner, the motion judge agreed to hear the Commonwealth's motion for reconsideration. See Commonwealth v. Dotson, 402 Mass. 185, 187 (1988) (judge has discretion to hear from the Commonwealth in motion brought under G.L. c. 261, §27C). The judge did not abuse her discretion, see Commonwealth v. Zagrodny, 443 Mass. 93, 108 (2004), in concluding that the defendant has not shown that a third expert is "reasonably necessary to prevent the party from being subjected to a disadvantage in preparing or presenting his case adequately, in comparison with one who could afford to pay for the preparation which the case reasonably requires." Lockley, supra. at 160-161. No transcript of the motion hearing has been provided, but at the hearing in this court the defendant confirmed that his second expert will opine on both mental abnormality and likelihood of re-offense. The Commonwealth is proceeding with one expert, the QE. The defendant will proceed with two (the QE and the retained expert). The defendant has not been disadvantaged. (Sullivan, J.). *Notice/Attest/Kenton-Walker, J.
