    11/16/2017 #13 ORDER: This notice is issued pursuant to Comment [5] to Rule 2.11 of the Code of Judicial Conduct, which states, "A judge should disclose on the record information that the judge believes the parties or their lawyers might reasonably consider relevant to a possible motion for disqualification, even if the judge believes there is no basis for disqualification." The Executive Office of Public Safety and Security (EOPSS) is named as a defendant in the matter underlying this appeal. Justice Massing served as General Counsel for EOPSS from 2007 through 2011. He did not participate in the matter in controversy, which occurred after his service with EOPSS ended, and did not express any opinion on the merits of the subject matter during his tenure at EOPSS. Justice Massing has no social relationships with any current employees at EOPSS or the Department of State Police and believes there is no basis for disqualification. Nonetheless, Justice Massing will recuse himself from this matter if any of the parties object to his continued involvement. If your client objects to Justice Massing's continued participation in the appeal, please respond to the Clerk's office, by letter via email at emotions@appct.state.ma.us, on or before November 27, 2017. (Massing, J.). Notice.
