    08/31/2017 #8 Memorandum and Order: ... "Because there was a timely objection at trial, it is clear that the counsel's failure to raise the courtroom closure issue on direct appeal resulted in prejudice; had he raised the issue on direct appeal, the defendant would have been entitled to an automatic reversal. See Weaver, supra at 1910, quoting Neder v. United States, 527 U.S. 1, 7 (1999). Counsel's failure to raise the issue effectively deprived the defendant of his best defense. Thus, the Commonwealth has not shown as abuse of discretion by the motion judge, nor any other substantial question that would entitle it to appeal. For the foregoing reasons, an order shall enter denying the Commonwealth's gatekeeper petition under G. L. c. 278, § 33E." (Budd, J.)
    08/31/2017 #9 ORDER: ... "In accordance with the Memorandum and Order of this date, it is ORDERED and ADJUDGED that the Commonwealth's application pursuant to G. L. c. 278, § 33E, for leave to appeal from the allowance of defendant's second motion for new trial, is DENIED." (Budd, J.)
