    03/02/2017 #5 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER: The defendant filed a petition pursuant to G. L. c. 231, § 118 (first par.) requesting that the single justice: "vacate . . . the order restricting father's right to file motions and other relevant documents freely . . ."; "overturn the denial and allow father's motion to compel response to defendant's first set of interrogatories and first and second request for production of documents;" (entered "overturn the denial and/or allow fathers motion to recuse judge Leilah K;" and "stay the case or trial date." The order allowing plaintiff's "Motion for Order Prohibiting Defendant's Filing of Motions Absent Advance Court Approval" was entered on October 15, 2015. Accordingly, the relief sought is denied, as the petition was not filed within thirty days of that order. The relief sought as to the order denying the motion to compel discovery is denied, as there has been no showing of abuse of discretion or error of law by the trial judge. As to the order denying the motion to recuse, given the petitioner's filing of criminal charges, it is remanded to the trial judge for entry of findings pursuant to Haddad v. Gonzalez, 410 Mass. 855, 862( 1991). The findings are to be prepared within fourteen days of the entry of this order, and a copy is to be transmitted to this court. The motion to stay the case or trial date is denied. By the Court (Desmond, J.). Notice/attest/Keamy, J.
    03/09/2017 #6 Letter from Worcester Probate and Family Court re: Relevant Procedural History and Findings of Fact.

    03/17/2017   ORDER: Upon review of the trial judge's "Relevant Procedural History and Findings of Fact" with regard to the denial of the motion to recuse, petitioner's request for relief from that order is denied. The motion for stay is denied. No further requests for relief are to be made in this matter as it is decided and closed. (Desmond, J.). *Notice/Attest/Keamy, J.
