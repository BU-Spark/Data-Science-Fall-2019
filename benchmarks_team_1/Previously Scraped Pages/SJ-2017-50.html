    03/07/2017 #5 ORDER: "No action will be taken on the plaintiff's complaint for relief in the nature of certiorari until the plaintiff has amended the complaint to include as defendants, and has made service on, the Commonwealth (represented by the Worcester District Attorney) and the codefendant in the underlying criminal litigation. They are the real parties in interest in this action; the judge who issued the challenged order is a nominal party only. See Lally v. Dorchester Div. of the Dist. Court Dep't, 26 Mass. App. Ct. 724, 728-729 (1988). See also Mass. R. Civ. P. 1 and 19 (a) (joinder of necessary parties). Cf. Supreme Judicial Court Rule 2:22 (same procedure applicable to petitions pursuant to G. L. c. 211, § 3). After they have been named and served, and have had an opportunity to answer the complaint, the court will be in a position to decide whether a certiorari action is a correct procedural route by which to seek relief in these circumstances, and if so, whether certiorari is warranted on these facts. Failure to amend the complaint and make service within thirty days will result in the dismissal of the action." (Hines, J.)

    05/26/2017 #10 JUDGMENT: ... "A writ of certiorari is not a correct procedural route by which to seek relief in these circumstances. Any appeal of an order of the trial court must be in compliance with G. L. c. 278, § 33E. It is ORDERED and ADJUDGED that the complaint filed pursuant to G. L. c. 249, §4 be, and the same hereby is, DISMISSED." (Lowy, J.)
