    10/19/2016 #9 Reservation and Report: ... "I reserve and report this case without decision for determination by the Supreme Judicial Court on the record now before me, limited to the review of the first issue -- whether the post-conviction case of a defendant who was tried on an indictment for murder in the first degree and was convicted of murder in the first degree, but who was a juvenile at the time o the crime and thus subject to a lesser penalty than life without the possibility of parole, is a "capital case" as defined in § 33E. See Patrick P. v. Commonwealth, 421 Mass. 186, 194 (1995), citing Commonwealth v. Angiulo, 415 Mass. 502, 507-510 (1993) ('determination of what constitutes a capital case takes into account not only the requirement of a first degree murder indictment, but also the possible severity of the punishment involved"). ... In their briefs before the full court, the parties shall address only the question of whether the defendant is subject to the gatekeeper provision of G. L. c. 278, 33E. Resolution of that issue will determine the applicability of the second issue. ... (Hines, J.)
    10/19/2016 #10 Notice of assembly of the record.
    10/19/2016 #11 Notice to counsel/parties, regarding paper #'s 9 & 10 filed.
    08/30/2017 #12 Rescript: "ORDERED, 'that the following entry be made in the docket; viz., - The reported question is answered as follows: the gatekeeper provision of G.L. c. 278, §33E, applies to a juvenile defendant who, like Steven James, has had a direct appeal, has received plenary review and, following that review, remains convicted of murder in the first degree. The case shall proceed in the county court for consideration of Jame's gatekeeper application, specifically whether the issues presented in his new trial motion are "new and substantial" for purposes of §33E. See Commonwealth v. Gunter, 459 Mass. 480, 487-488, cert. denied, 565 U.S. 868 (2011).
