    02/07/2018 #23 Joint Motion For Reservation And Report with Certificate of Service and Exhibit A filed by AAG Seth Schofield, Atty. John DeTore, Atty. Seth Jaffe, and Atty. Nicholas Scobbo, Jr. (SEE PAPER #24)
    02/09/2018 #24 Reservation and Report: " This matter came before the court, Budd, J., pursuant to an order of transfer, on petitioners New England Power Generators Association and GenOn Energy, Inc.'s (NEPGA) complaint for judicial review pursuant to G.L. c. 30A, § 7, and G.L. c. 231A, §§ 1-2, of the regulations promulgated by respondents Massachusetts Department of Environmental Protection and the Executive Office of Energy and Environmental Affairs (the Agencies) and codified at 310 C.M.R. § 7.74.[1] Following a hearing at which the parties indicated their joint desire to reserve and report the matter, the parties filed a written joint motion for reservation and report.[2] Upon consideration thereof, the joint motion of NEPGA and the Agencies requesting reservation and report of SJ-2018-45 be, and hereby is, ALLOWED. Accordingly, the complaint for judicial review shall be reserved and reported to the full for determination on the record before the Single Justice in SJ-2018-45. The record in SJ-2018-0045 is comprised of the following: 1. The Complaint of NEPGA for judicial review under G.L. c. 30A, § 7, and G.L. c. 231A, §§ 1-2, which was filed by NEPGA in the Suffolk Superior Court on September 11, 2017 and docketed as 1684-CV-02918; 2. The Stipulation to Answer by Filing the Administrative Record, which was filed in 1684-CV-02918 on October 17, 2017; 3. The order of the Suffolk Superior Court (Wilkins, J.) granting Proposed-Intervenor-Plaintiff Footprint Power Salem Harbor Development L.P.'s (Footprint) Motion to Intervene, dated December 5, 2017; 4. The Answer of the Agencies, dated December 7, 2017; 5. The certified copy of the Administrative Record for the regulations Reducing CO2 Emissions from Electricity Generating Facilities, 310 C.M.R. § 7.74, and the Clean Energy Standard, 310 C.M.R. § 7.75, filed by the Agencies in the Superior Court on December 7, 2017; 6. The Intervention Complaint of Footprint Power Salem Harbor Development L.P. (Footprint) for judicial review under G.L. c. 30A, § 7, and G.L. c. 231A, §§ 1-9, which was filed in 1684-CV-02918 on December 11, 2017; 7. The order of the Suffolk Superior Court (Wilkins, J.) granting Proposed-Intervenor-Plaintiff Massachusetts Municipal Wholesale Electric Company's (MMWEC) Conditionally Unopposed Motion to Intervene, dated December 13, 2017; 8. The Order of Transfer (Budd, J.), dated January 31, 2018; 9. A suitable statement of agreed facts to be prepared jointly by the parties and included in the record appendix. 10. The docket sheet for Superior Court Civil Action 1684-CV-02918; 11. The docket sheet for SJ-2018-0045; and 12. This Reservation and Report. On this Reservation and Report, NEPGA and intervenors Footprint and MMWEC shall be designated the appellants and appellant-intervenors before the full court, and the Agencies shall be designated the appellees. This reservation and report shall proceed according to the following schedule. The appellees shall serve and file within seven (7) days of the date on which this matter is entered on the docket of the full court the Record Appendix, which will consist of (i) the complete Administrative Record referenced in paragraph 5 above in volumes 1 through 5, and (ii) the documents listed above in paragraphs 1 through 4 and 6 through 12 in volume(s) 6 and 7 (if needed). The appellants and appellant-intervenors shall serve and file their brief(s) on February 27, 2018. The appellants and appellant-intervenors shall coordinate their filing(s) to eliminate, to the extent possible, unnecessary repetition on the issues presented to the Court for review. The appellees shall serve and file their brief(s) in response to the appellants and appellant-intervenors brief(s) on April 13, 2018. The appellants and appellant-intervenors shall serve and file their reply brief(s) on April 27, 2018. The parties shall consult with the Clerk of the Supreme Judicial Court for the Commonwealth as needed concerning the number of briefs filed by the parties, the consolidation of any briefs, and the length of the briefs. The Conservation Law Foundation and any other interested entities and individuals may file amicus briefs no later than April 13, 2018. Oral argument will be scheduled during the May sitting of the full Court. This Reservation and Report shall proceed in all other respects in conformance with the Massachusetts Rules of Appellate Procedure. [1] This matter originally was filed in the Suffolk Superior Court and docketed as case no. 1784CV02918. [2] The parties' joint motion for reservation and report was joined by the parties in three related Single Justice matters concerning the petitioner Conservation Law Foundation's consolidated petitions for review of decisions of the Energy Facilities Siting Board pursuant to G.L. c. 164, § 69P, and G.L. c. 25, § 5. These three consolidated matters are SJ-2016-0509, SJ-2017-0290, and SJ-2017-0328. The joint motion for reservation and report requested that all four related matters be reserved and reported simultaneously and then sequenced such that SJ-2018-045 would proceed first to argument before the full Court and after its decision, the remaining three matters would proceed to argument before the full Court. Administrative concerns and conformance with historical practice, however, suggest that reserving and reporting only SJ-2018-045 at this time, while staying the other three matters pending the full Court's decision concerning SJ-2018-045, is the better practice. It is this court's intention to reserve and report expeditiously so much, if any, of the remaining three consolidated matters as may be necessary, after the full court issues its decision on the reservation and report of SJ-2018-045." (Budd, J.)
