    02/06/2013 #4 ORDER: Bar counsel filed a notice of conviction and petition for reciprocal discipline in the county court, alleging that, on June 26, 1999, following a guilty plea, the respondent attorney had been convicted in the United States District Court, Southern District of Florida, of conspiracy to possess with intent to distribute cocaine, in violation of 21 U.S.C. § 841(a)(1). In addition, bar counsel alleged that on November 12, 1999, the Florida Supreme Court granted the respondent's petition for disciplinary resignation from the Florida bar, and granted him leave to seek readmission to the Florida bar after five years. Bar counsel further alleged that the respondent did not notify bar counsel of either his conviction or his resignation, in violation of S.J.C. Rule 4:01, §§ 12(8) and 16(6). After a hearing, the single justice declined to impose identical reciprocal discipline, ordering instead that the respondent be suspended for the practice of law for a period of three years, effective on the entry of her order. Bar counsel appeals. Following the procedure established by this court for appeals in bar discipline cases, bar counsel has filed a preliminary memorandum and record appendix. See Order Establishing a Modified Procedure for Appeals in Bar Discipline Cases, Massachusetts Rules of Court 469-470 (West 2012). Based on our review of bar counsel's preliminary memorandum and record appendix, the court invites the respondent to file a responsive memorandum, not to exceed twenty pages, double spaced, within twenty days.(By the Court) (Lenk, J. recused.) Notice sent.

    06/28/2013 #6 ORDER (By the Court): After review of the parties' memoranda, it is ordered that the appeal will proceed in the regular course.
