    12/27/2017 #28 ORDER: Defendant Lauren Hodges appeals from an order of the single justice denying her petition for a stay of execution of a judgment pending her merits-based appeal. In the underlying summary process matter the judgment entered on December 16, 2016, awarding possession to plaintiff Mikkel Thorup. Hodges filed a petition for a stay of execution of the judgment with a single justice of this court, who denied Hodges's petition on March 3, 2017. The record reflects that after Hodges noticed her appeal from the single justice's decision Thorup obtained an order allowing him to execute on the judgment. Officers thereafter forcibly removed Hodges and her belongings from the premises. Because Thorup has executed on the underlying judgment and is now in possession, Hodges's effort to stay execution of the judgment -- the limited matter now before us -- is moot because we no longer are able to afford Hodges meaningful relief. See Petrillo-Aufiero v. Petrillo, 436 Mass. 1002, 1002 (2002). We express no opinion on the merits of the underlying appeal. Appeal from single justice order dismissed. (Vuono, Sullivan & Massing, JJ.[1]) *Notice/attest Footnote [1] The panelists are listed in order of seniority.
