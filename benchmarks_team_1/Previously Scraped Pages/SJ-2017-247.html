    02/13/2018 #16 ORDER: "The defendant's January 22, 2018 request for reciprocal assistance of the court is allowed. While the procedural course of this appeal has been somewhat unusual, the reasons that I allowed the appeal to proceed are not. Whether Massachusetts will retain the common law rule on abatement generally, and if so, whether a conviction should be abated in specific circumstances where the Commonwealth maintains that the defendant took his own life, are important questions for the appellate court to decide, and the latter question in particular appears to be novel. The appellate court and the parties will benefit from a full record and robust advocacy. The defendant is therefore granted permission to file a notice of cross-appeal at this time from Judge Garsh's rulings on the breadth of the documentary information she considered when ruling on the matter. Additionally, if the Commonwealth insists on pressing the point made by Judge McGuire when he struck the defendant's first notice of cross-appeal, the parties may argue before the appellate court whether a defendant, represented by his criminal counsel, may continue to defend an abatement of his conviction on appeal and to cross-appeal from related rulings made in the criminal proceeding, or whether the defendant's estate is the true party in interest. The defendant's cross-appeal should be entered in the Appeals Court under the same docket number as the Commonwealth's appeal, 18-P-87, or if entered under a different number, should be consolidated with 18-P-87 for purposes of briefing and argument." (Lowy, J.)
