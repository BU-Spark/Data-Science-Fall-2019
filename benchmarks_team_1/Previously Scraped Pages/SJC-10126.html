    07/27/2009 #21 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER (By the Court): In Comm. v. Mateo-German...this court reversed the Superior Court judge's decision allowing the defendant's motion to suppress and remanded the case for further proceedings. The defendant's petition for rehearing was denied on 7/9/09. Purusant to MRCrimP 15(d)...the defendant has filed a motion for costs incurred due to the Commonwealth's appeal. This motion is accompanied by his counsel's affidavit detailing the services provided and the rate charged. The defendant seeks costs of $32,940.11, including $32,771.25 for 145.65 hours of his counsel's work on this appeal at $225 per hour and $168.86 in copying and transportation expenses. The Commonwealth has filed an opposition to the defendant's motion in which it states that the number of hours charged is excessive, the defendant's attorney has not adequately justified his hourly rrate, and attorneys in four unrelated cases sought lower hourly rates than that requested in this case. In a fifth case cited by the Commonwealth, the attorney sought an hourly rate of $250. The defendant is entitled to an award of attorney's fees and costs in this case...and the defendant has complied with the procedure for requesting such an award.... Therefore, we consider only the reasonableness of the rate charged and the time spent. In his affidavit, the defendant's counsel states that, since becoming a member of the bar in 1996, he has specialized in criminal appeals. He further states that, for purposes of this appeal, he has substantially lowered his usual hourly rate in order to reduce the financial burden on the Commonwealth. He has not, however, stated what his usual hourly rate is. Based on our review of fee awards in cases similar to the present one, the rate billed for this appeal, $225 per hour, is reasonable. The total number of hours charged, on the other hand, is excessive. Most significantly, the amount of time expended on researching and writing the defendant's twenty-page appellate brief is far from reasonable. The defendant asserts that his counsel spent 16.2 hours reviewing the record and the Commonwealth's filings, 20.6 hours researching case law, and 58.5 hours writing and editing the brief. Althought he brief is well-written and on point, it should not have required 95.3 hours to research and write. First, the entire four-page fact section of the brief merely quoted the motion judge's findings of fact. Moreover, while two of the issues raised int he brief involved novel questions concerning a canine drug sniff during a community caretaking function, the same questions had been addressed previously at the suppression hearing. In addition, the defendant relied heavily on one particular Mass. case, Comm. v. Feyenord..., cited by the motion judge in his decision allowing the motion to suppress. Given very experienced appellate counsel who practices only in the area of criminal law, a question concerning a motion to suppress drugs found in a motor vehicle should not have required this level of effort.... The hours should have been adequate to review the Commonwealth's 22 page brief and the transcript of the suppression hearing, and 40 hours would have been a reasonable amount of time to draft and edit the brief. (fn1) In addition the time sought includes 7.2 hours to draft a petition for rehearing which merely resated all of the arguments previously made. The time spent on this petition was unreasonable, see T & D Video, Inc. v. Revere..., and we reduce this time to two hours. The time charged also includes 4.25 hours to draft the one-paragraph motion for costs, and three-paragraph supporting affidavit, and the four pages containing the listing of services provided. This list sets forth the date of the work performed, a description consisting of a few words, and the amount of time spent performing the listed task. Extraction of this information from the attorney's time logs and drafting of the motion should not have required more than one hour. The copying costs of $144.86 and the transportation cost sof $24 for bus fare from Barnstable are reasonable. Accordingly, the defendant's request is allowed in the amount reflecting reasonable hours expended on the Commonwealth's appeal. The defendant is awarded $25,312.50 in attorney's fees and $168.86 in costs, for a total of $25,481.36....This order shall be enforced in all respects by the Bristol Superior Court. (Notice to counsel and Bristol Superior Court.)
