    11/07/2017 #3 Docketing Statement received from Chukwudera Okoli.
    11/14/2017   ORDER: The defendant filed a notice of appeal from an order of the Suffolk Probate and Family Court, which was entered on its docket on May 10, 2017. Pursuant to Mass. R. A. P. 4(a), the notice of appeal must be filed within thirty days from the date of the entry of the judgment, decree, order or adjudication. In this case, the notice of appeal was not filed until August 11, 2017. Pursuant to Mass. R. A. P. 4(c), the lower court may extend the time for filing the notice of appeal by any party for a period not to exceed thirty days from the expiration of the time otherwise prescribed by this rule. The defendant filed a motion to enlarge the time to file the notice of appeal on July 19, 2017 -- sixty-nine days after the date of entry of the order. On or before 12/1/17, the appellant shall (1) move for voluntary dismissal of the above-captioned appeal pursuant to Mass. R.App. P. 29 (b); (2) show cause, in writing, why jurisdiction over this appeal is proper notwithstanding the apparently late notice of appeal; or (3) move for leave to file a late notice of appeal from the order. *Notice/Attest.
    11/22/2017 #5 Response to order dated 11/14/17 filed by Chukwudera Okoli.
    11/28/2017   ORDER: Notwithstanding the trial court's allowance on 7/19/17 of the defendant's motion to file a late notice of appeal from the 5/3/17 decision and extending the time for filing until 8/19/17, the lower court may only extend the time for filing the notice of appeal "for a period not to exceed thirty days from the expiration of the time otherwise prescribed by this rule. " See Mass. R. App. Proc. 4(c). After that time, a single justice of the Appeals Court, for good cause shown, may extend the time for filing a notice of appeal for up to one year from the date of entry of judgment. See Mass. R. App. Proc. 14(b). Accordingly, the defendant is to further show cause, in writing, whether the trial court had authority to enlarge the time for filing a notice of appeal, or in the alternative, may file a motion for enlargement of time to file a notice of appeal in 17-P-1394. The failure to take action by 12/5/17 may lead to dismissal of this appeal for lack of prosecution. *Notice/Attest/Dunn, J.
