In a handicap discrimination case in which an employer acknowledges reliance on an employee's physical impairments for its employment decision, for purposes of G. L. c. 151B, § 4 (16): (1) what are the parties' respective burdens of proof and production with regard to the determination whether the employee is a "qualified handicapped person" who has been subjected to an adverse employment action because of his or her handicap; and (2) whether the burden-shifting framework established in McDonnell Douglas Corp. v. Green, 411 U.S. 792 (1973), applies to a claim of handicap discrimination in such a case.
