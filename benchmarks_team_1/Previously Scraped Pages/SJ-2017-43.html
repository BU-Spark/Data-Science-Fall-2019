    01/31/2017 #6 JUDGMENT: " This matter came before the Court, Lowy, J., on the tenant's emergency petition asking this Court to stay the execution for possession of the premises at 15 University Road, Apt. #33, Brookline, Massachusetts, scheduled for January 31, 2017. The landlord has filed a response. The Summary Process began in the Brookline District Court in 2012. Following a decision in favor of the landlord, the tenant has taken appeals to the Appellate Division, to the Appeals Court, and ultimately a request for further appellate review from the Supreme Judicial Court. All were denied. Following the service of the execution, the tenant went before the Brookline District Court for a stay. That stay was denied on December 30, 2016, Pomarole, J. On January 5, 2017 the parties entered into a written agreement which was reviewed before, and endorsed by the Brookline District Court, White, J. In the agreement, the tenant agrees, among other things, to vacate the premises and remove her possessions no later than 10:00 PM on January 31, 2017. She also waives any further appeals or stays. The tenant has provided an affidavit to waive the fee, albeit not signed. Because of the exigent circumstances, the fee is waived. The clerks are further instructed to use the caption of the Brookline District Court, as required by our practice. The tenant has filed another motion seeking further appellate review, and to have the previous courts' proceedings impounded or sealed. Further appellate review is a function of the full court, not the single justice, but it is noted that such review was denied on November 4, 2016 (See FAR-24741.) Matters of impoundment must first be addressed to the court where the case originated. Finally, the tenant has filed here citing Mass. R.A.P. Rule 15 (a) and (b). The Rules of Appellate Procedure apply to matters before the full Supreme Judicial Court or the full Appeals Court. Treating the petition as filed pursuant to G.L. c. 211, § 3, as a request for the Court to exercise its extraordinary power under that statute, and upon consideration of the pleadings of the parties, it is ORDERED that petition, including all requests for relief, be, and hereby is, DENIED without hearing."
