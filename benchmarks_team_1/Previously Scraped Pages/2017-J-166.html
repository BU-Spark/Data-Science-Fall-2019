    05/12/2017 #9 ORDER: The plaintiff prevailed in a summary process action against the defendants in the Worcester Housing Court. The defendants timely filed a notice of appeal from the judgment of possession. Thereafter, the Housing Court judge denied the defendants' motion to waive the appeal bond and entered an order establishing the bond in the amount of $50,000 and requiring use and occupancy payments of $4,000 per month, due on the first day of each month starting May 1, 2017. This single justice matter is limited to the defendants' appeal of the amount of the bond and periodic payments under G. L. c. 239, § 5. Nevertheless, the defendants have argued that the underlying summary process judgment should be vacated for lack of subject matter jurisdiction and other reasons. I decline to reach those issues, as the validity of the summary process judgment is not before me. The defendants are free to assert their claims of jurisdictional and other defects in their full appeal from the summary process judgment. With respect to the bond appeal, this court is required to "review the findings, the amount of the bond or deposit, if any, and the amount of period payment required, if any, as if it were initially deciding the matter, and the court may withdraw or amend any finding or reduce or rescind any amount of bond, deposit or periodic payment when in its judgment the facts so warrant." G. L. c. 239, § 5(f). The facts before me warrant the periodic payments of $4,000 per month, as the judge ordered. Accordingly, the defendants are to begin making periodic payments forthwith; that is, $4,000 immediately, $4,000 on June 1, and so on, while the appeal is pending. However, on the record before me, I am unable to determine whether a bond in the amount of $50,000 is warranted. Accordingly, the matter is remanded to the Housing Court judge for findings of fact and her rationale for the amount of the bond, to be transmitted to this court on or before May 26, 2017. Except as to the ordered periodic payments, this appeal is stayed pending receipt and review of the judge's findings and rationale (Massing, J.). *Notice/Attest/Horan, J.
