    08/30/2016   ORDER: This Court received a notice of assembly of the record following a request for review of an appeal bond order in Worcester Housing Court docket number 15H85SP004099, Federal Home Loan Corp. v. Evans. The "notice of required appeal bond" issued by the clerk of the Housing Court states that the bond in question is based on the requirements of G.L. c. 239, ss. 5 and 6. Upon receipt, pursuant to the summary process statute, this Court scheduled a hearing for Wednesday, August 31, 2016. In reviewing the paperwork transmitted by the Housing Court, it became clear that this is not "an appeal . . . from a judgment of . . . a housing court." G.L. c. 239, s. 5(c). Instead, it is an appeal from an order denying post-judgment relief. Therefore, it is not clear that an appeal bond should have been established pursuant to the summary process statute. However, if G.L. c. 239, s. 5 applies to this matter, as the Housing Court indicated in its order, notice of order, and transmission of the record, it is clear that dismissing the appeal for failure to pay the bond prior to the single justice's review of the bond order is a clear error of law or abuse of discretion, regardless of whether the defendant appeared at a hearing on the motion to dismiss the appeal for failure to comply with the bond. Thus, the hearing scheduled for August 31, 2016 is cancelled. Upon the filing in the Housing Court of a notice of appeal from August 23, 2016 order dismissing the defendant's appeal, the defendant may seek a stay pending appeal in the Housing Court. If the Housing Court denies the defendant's request for a stay, she may then file her motion in this docket in the Appeals Court. I will retain jurisdiction over any such motion. The defendant shall file a status report on or before September 5, 2016. (Carhart, J.). *Notice/Attest/Horan, J.
