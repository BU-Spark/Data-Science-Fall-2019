    06/06/2008 #4 ORDER (By the Court): Graham Chubbuck appeals from the denial of his petition under GLc211, s3, by a single justice of this court, in which he sought relief from the denial of his motion to dismiss certain criminal charges against him pursuant to GLc123, s16(f). That statute requires the dismissal of charges pending against an incompetent person on the date that he would have been eligible for parole "if he had been convicted of the most serious crime with which he was charged in court and sentenced to the maximum sentence he would have received, if so convicted."....The case is before us pursuant to SJC Rule 2:21...which requires Chubbuck to "set forth the reasons why review of the trial court decision cannot adequately be obtained on appeal from any final adverse judgment in the trial court or by other available means." Although Chubbuck has not complied with the requirement that he file "a memorandum...in which the appellant sets forth the reasons why review of the trial court decisin cannot adequately be obtained on appeal...or by other means," we nonetheless conclude that the appeal may proceed in the ordinary course. (fn1: Chubbuck filed a set of copies of documents that had been filed in the county court, but he did not file the required memorandum. "The purpose of this requirement is to focus the court's and counsel's attention on the narrow question whether, regardless of the merits of the substantive claim of error, the petitioner has an adequate remedy apart from resort to GLc211, s3.:....) If Chubbuck regains his competency and is tried, he can raise the denial of his motion to dismiss on appeal from any conviction. However, if Chubbuck does not regain his competency, he will be unable to do so. Further, although the Commonwealth suggested in the county court that the charges are due to be dismissed under s16(f) in 2015, we are not convinced that this would be an adequate remedy for the failure to dismiss the charges in 2007 (assuming that is the correct s16(f) dismissal date, as Chubbuck argues). Chubbuck's appeal from the single justice's decision shall proceed in the ordinary course. (Notice to counsel.)

    02/04/2009 #11 ORDER (By the Court): In an earlier order, we authorized Graham Chubbuck (petitioner) to proceed in the regular course with his appeal from a judgment of a single justice of this court denying his petition for relief under G. L. c. 211, s.3. See SJC Rule 2:21, as amended, 434 Mass. 1301 (2001). The parties have since filed their briefs. At the rule 2:21 stage, the petitioner filed only a record appendix of the county court proceedings and failed to submit a memorandum setting forth the reasons why "review of the trial court decision(s) cannot adequately be obtained on appeal from any final adverse judgment in the trial court or by other available means." Rule 2:21(2). Now that the case has been fully briefed, however, it is clear to us that the only relief the petitioner seeks is dismissal of the complaint for indecent assaults and batteries -- he does not seek dismissal of the complaint for breaking and entering. It is also clear to us at this time that the petitioner has been found incompetent to stand trial on both the complaint for indecent assaults and batteries and the complaint for breaking and entering, and that he is currently confined on each complaint independently, pending restoration of his competency on each complaint. In light of these facts, we direct the parties to submit supplemental memoranda, not to exceed ten pages per side, addressing the threshold question of the propriety of invoking this court's extraordinary superintendence power under G. L. c. 211, s.3 -- i.e., the presence or absence of adequate alternative remedies. The memoranda shall be filed within thirty days of the date of this order. The case shall be heard at the court's April sitting. (Notice to counsel.)
