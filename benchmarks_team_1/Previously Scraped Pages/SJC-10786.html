    07/05/2011 #16 ORDER (By the Court): This case involves a challenge to the constitutionality of the new Massachusetts Child Support Guidelines. The plaintiffs seek declaratory and injunctive relief against the Chief Justice for Administrative Management and the justices of the Trial Court of Massachusetts. The court seeks the parties' views as to whether the language in the declaratory judgment act, G. L. c. 231A, § 2, stating that the section "shall not apply to the governor and council or the legislative and judicial departments" precludes this action. The court therefore directs each side to submit a supplemental memorandum, not to exceed fifteen pages, addressing this issue. All supplemental memoranda shall be filed by 4 P.M. on Monday, July 18.

    09/01/2011 #20 RESCRIPT (Full Opinion): Because declaratory relief pursuant to G. L. c. 231A, is barred by the plain language of the statute, and as the plaintiffs will have an opportunity to challenge the new Massachusetts Child Support Guidelines as applied in their individual cases, we affirm the Superior Court judge's dismissal of the plaintiffs' complaint. Judgment affirmed. (By the Court). Reasons as on file.
