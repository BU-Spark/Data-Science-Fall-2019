    02/27/2013 #7 ORDER: Appeal allowed to proceed. This is the petitioner's appeal from an order of a single justice that, in essence, affirmed a decision by the Board of Registration in Pharmacy suspending the petitioner's license. The petitioner has filed a five-page Memorandum of Appeal, with appendix, along with a motion to proceed on the original record. It appears that the petitioner, who is representing himself, may be under the mistaken impression that his appeal is governed by S.J.C. Rule 2:21, as amended, 434 Mass. 1301 (20001), which it is not. Rule 2:21 applies only when a single justice denies relief from a challenged interlocutory ruling in the trial court. The matter at issue before the single justice here was a final decision and order of the board. This appeal shall proceed in the regular course, pursuant to the Massachusetts Rules of Appellate Procedure. The petitioner's brief (in compliance with Mass. R. A. P. 16) will be due forty days after the date of this order. The motion to proceed on the original record is hereby allowed. Notice sent.
