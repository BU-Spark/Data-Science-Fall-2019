    04/11/2017 #43 ORDER: The plaintiff's request for costs and attorney's fees incurred in the proceedings before this court is hereby allowed, although not in the full amount requested. Having carefully reviewed the plaintiff's petition, her supporting material, the defendants' opposition, the plaintiff's reply and the defendants' sur-reply, the application for direct appellate review, as well as the briefs and other materials that have been filed in this court, we conclude that a reduction in the amount of fees is appropriate. See Stowe v. Bologna, 417 Mass. 199, 203-204 (1994); Linthicum v. Archambault, 379 Mass. 381, 388 (1979) (considering 'nature of the case and the issues presented, the time and labor required, the amount of the damages involved, the result obtained, the experience, reputation and ability of the attorney, the usual price charged for similar services by other attorneys in the same area, and the amount of awards in similar cases'). Although the factual record was extensive, counsel has represented the plaintiff throughout the proceedings -- in the trial court as well as in this court -- and was therefore thoroughly familiar with the facts and the law of the case before the appeal. With the exception of one set of issues, the law was not complex given the experience of the attorneys. Although the plaintiff is entitled to fees and costs, the amount that counsel is seeking -- $101,889.75 in fees and $11,555.01 in costs, for a total of $113,444.76 -- should be reduced in the circumstances. While counsel prepared several different documents, including an application for direct appellate review, a brief, and an opposition, there was a fair amount of overlap between some of those documents, particularly the application and the brief. Additionally, some of the work performed by attorneys either duplicated efforts, or reasonably could have been performed by persons who were not attorneys. Accordingly, the plaintiff's petition for fees will be allowed in the amount of $81,000, and costs are to be awarded in the amount of $11,555.01. Interest shall accrue from the date of entry of this order. This order may be enforced, if necessary, by the Superior Court. By the Court
