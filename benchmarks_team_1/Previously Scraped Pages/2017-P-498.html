    06/21/2017 #7 OPPOSITION to paper #6, filed by Gerald J. Pacelli.
    06/21/2017   RE#6: After review of the motion and opposition, the motion is allowed to the extent that the Land Court's Warrant to the Partition Commissioner dated March 31, 2017, and its Order and Notice to Vincent Pacelli to Quit and Vacate, and Authorization of Eviction, also dated March 31, 2017, are stayed pending further order of this court. I concur with the motion judge's conclusion that Vincent Pacelli will suffer irreparable harm absent a stay pending appeal. I am less persuaded of the likelihood that a stay will result in the parties receiving a lesser price if the property is ultimately sold as a result of the Land Court judgment being affirmed in whole or part on appeal. Most important, I view Vincent Pacelli as having a stronger likelihood of success on the merits of his appeal than did the motion judge. See C.E. v. J.E., 472 Mass. 1016, 1017 (2015). In particular, it appears that Paragraph 6 of the 2003 Sales Agreement is most naturally construed as requiring the agreement only of the "surviving" beneficial interest holders (currently consisting of the four Pacelli brothers as tenants in common) before the property may be sold. To construe Paragraph 5 of the Sales Agreement (requiring successors and heirs of the beneficial interest holders and of their three sisters to adhere to the Sales Agreement) as also requiring the agreement of such successors and heirs before the property may be sold would render the word "surviving" in Paragraph 6 superfluous, contrary to accepted principles of contract interpretation. Paragraph 5 appears to entitle any such successors and heirs to a share of the proceeds of any sale, but not (to adopt the parties' and the Land Court's shorthand terminology) to "veto" the sale itself. Construing the Sales Agreement as conferring such "veto" power only upon those of the four Pacelli brothers who remain alive at the time of any contemplated sale would mean that the restriction on the sale of the property is limited in duration and thus not unenforceable under Franklin v. Spadafora, 388 Mass. 764, 766-767 (1983). Such a construction draws support from the "well settled principle that a construction rendering a contract valid and enforceable is to be preferred to one which makes it void or its performance impossible or meaningless." Talbot v. Rednalloh Co., 283 Mass. 225, 230 (1933). Although the panel of this court that hears this appeal may ultimately disagree with my view of the merits, I conclude that Vincent Pacelli's showing of likelihood of success, considered together with the balance of harms, warrants the requested stay. (Sack, J.). *Notice/Attest/Vhay, J/Sands, J.
