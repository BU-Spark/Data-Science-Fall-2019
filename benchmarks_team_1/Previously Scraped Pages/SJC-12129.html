    06/29/2016 #2 SERVICE of Mem. & Appx. under Rule 2:21 for Plaintiff/Appellant Rockland Trust Company by Jason W. Morgan, W. Prescott Golding.
    09/30/2016 #3 ORDER: The petitioner, Rockland Trust Co., appeals from the single justice's denial of its petition under G. L. c. 211, § 3, in which it sought relief from the District Court judge's denial of its motion to dismiss. The limited question before us at this juncture, pursuant to S.J.C. Rule 2:21 (2), is whether the petitioner has demonstrated that "review of the trial court decision cannot adequately be obtained on appeal from any final adverse judgment in the trial court or by other available means." The petitioner relies heavily on our decision in Sperounes v. Farese, 449 Mass. 800 (2007), to make that showing. In that case we considered a somewhat similar legal issue: whether a judge in the District Court has discretion to permit a case to proceed to trial in the District Court where there is no reasonable likelihood that the damages would be below the $25,000 statutory threshold. The question in this case, essentially, is whether the same general principles apply to a counterclaim in the District Court. We made it clear in the Sperounes opinion that we were electing to consider that case on the merits pursuant to the second paragraph of G. L. c. 211, § 3, because it raised a systemic issue of some importance following the then-recent enactment of the Statewide one-trial system for civil cases. See id. at 802. See also St. 2004, c. 252. We did not hold in that case that every denial of a motion to dismiss based on the statutory amount is necessarily reviewable as a matter of right under G. L. c. 211, § 3. We will allow this appeal to proceed with briefing and argument so that we can consider that important preliminary question, i.e., whether a party whose motion to dismiss a claim or counterclaim in the District Court -- based on the $25,000 statutory amount -- is denied, is necessarily entitled as a matter of right to review of that ruling pursuant to G. L. c. 211, § 3. The parties should be sure to brief that preliminary question in addition to briefing the substantive merits of the issue raised by the petition, i.e., the applicability of Sperounes and Mass. R. Civ. P. 12 (b) (10) to counterclaims. The case is tentatively scheduled for the court's February sitting. The plaintiff's brief is due forty days from the date of this order, and the defendant's brief is due thirty days thereafter. See Mass. R. A. P. 19 (a). Extensions should not be anticipated.
