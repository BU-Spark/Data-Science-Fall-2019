    06/07/2017 #3 ORDER: The defendants filed this petition under G. L. c. 231, ~ 118, first para., for interlocutory relief from the endorsement of the plaintiff's motion to allow recording of a memorandum of lis pendens with respect to two contiguous parcels located at 123 Felton Street, Marlborough (locus). A judge of the Superior Court endorsed the memorandum on the ground that the "[p]laintiff has articulated some interest in the real estate attachment.". A party is entitled to the endorsement of a memorandum of lis pendens "if the subject matter of the action constitutes a claim of a right to title to real property or the use and occupancy thereof." G. L. c. 184, ~ 15(b) "[T]he allowance or denial of a memorandum of lis pendens hinges on the nature of the claim, not the merits thereof." Decroteau v. Decroteau, 90 Mass. App. Ct. 903, 905 (2016). It is undisputed, as the plaintiff's complaint alleges, that defendant 123 Felton Street LCC "holds legal title to the locus." The plaintiff contends that the defendants owe him "wages," and that the compensation owed to him includes "an economic interest" in the locus. Specifically, he seeks an amendment to the 123 Felton Street LLC operating agreement to give him a 33% share of the LLC's profit interest in the locus. The plaintiff is not claiming a right to title to the locus or to use or occupy the locus. The LLC holds title to the locus, and were the plaintiff to prevail in this lawsuit, the LLC would still hold title. Thus, the plaintiff's claim does not "affect title" to the real property within the meaning of G. L. c. 184, ~15. See Wolfe v. Gormally, 440 Mass. 699 (2004). Were the plaintiff to prevail, only the ownership of the LLC would be affected. Accordingly, the defendants' petition for relief is allowed. The order allowing the endorsement of the memorandum of lis pendens is reversed, and a new order shall enter dissolving the memorandum of lis pendens[2]. *Notice/Attest/Barrett, J.
