    05/26/2017 #8 ORDER: ... "on petitioner's motion for leave to file a late notice of appeal. This Court denied petitioner's original request to dismiss the indictment against him on January 27, 2017, noting that petitioner had relief available to him in the trial court. Petitioner did then seek relief in the trial court, which was denied on March 24, 2017, Roach, RAJ. Petitioner now seeks leave to late file the notice of appeal of that Superior Court decision. The trial court may extend the time for filing the notice of appeal for a period not to exceed thirty days from the expiration of the time otherwise prescribed by Mass. R. App. P. 4. Where the time for filing in the trial court has expired, Mass. R.A.P. 14(b), as amended, 378 Mass. 939 (1979), provides that the appellate court and its single justice may enlarge the time for filing a notice of appeal, but not beyond one year. As noted in the January 27, 2017 ruling in this matter, this Court will generally decline to act where other relief is available. Petitioner has a remedy in the Appeals Court. The motion is DENIED." (Lowy, J.)
