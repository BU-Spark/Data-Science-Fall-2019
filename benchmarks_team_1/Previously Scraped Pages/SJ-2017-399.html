    10/24/2017 #9 Memorandum of Decision... " The petitioner now seeks relief under G. L. c. 211 § 3, asking for interlocutory review of his double jeopardy claim and for a stay of the trial proceedings pending such review. The petition does not include a trial transcript, and the petitioner has indicated that he does not intend to provide one. A criminal defendant who raises a double jeopardy claim "of substantial merit" is entitled to review of that claim under G. L. c. 211 § 3. Neverson v. Commonwealth, 406 Mass. 174, 175 (1989). In order to review such a claim, however, the single justice must have an adequate factual record allowing him or her "to make an intelligent decision whether the Commonwealth had presented sufficient evidence to warrant submission of the case to the jury." Id. at 177. See also Hanlon v. Commonwealth, 419 Mass. 1005, 1005 (1995). It is the petitioner's burden, as appellant, to provide the record. See Commonwealth v. Montaz, 388 Mass. 603, 604 (1983). Without a trial transcript, the record here is "so deficient that we are in no position to rule on the sufficiency of the evidence." Id. As such, the petitioner has failed to raise a double jeopardy claim of substantial merit and is not entitled to review under G. L. c. 211 § 3. Accordingly, he is also not entitled to a stay. It is therefore ORDERED that his petition for interlocutory review of his double jeopardy claim and for a stay of the trial pending such review is DENIED." (Gants, C.J.)
