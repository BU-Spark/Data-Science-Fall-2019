    08/01/2017 #5 ORDER: After hearing and upon consideration of the record materials transmitted by the housing court pertaining to this appeal, the order denying the defendant Gerald Hughes's motion for waiver of the appeal bond under G. L. c. 239, § 5, is AFFIRMED. The record does not establish any error in the motion judge's conclusion that the defendant has no defense that is not frivolous [2]. At the hearing before me, the defendant argued that the underlying foreclosure was not valid, but he did not raise that argument in a timely manner below, as the judge concluded in her order denying the defendant's motion under Mass.R.Civ.P.60(b)(4) to vacate the judgment. Thus, the argument is not preserved and does not present a nonfrivolous issue for appeal. See G. L. c. 239, § 5(e). To the extent the defendant challenges the amount of the bond, there is nothing in the record that indicates clear error on the part of the judge. (Shin, J.) *Notice/Attest. Footnotes: 1. Arthur Hughes, Amanda Hughes, and Gerald Hughes. Only Gerald Hughes has filed a notice of appeal. 2. The motion judges was the same judge who entered summary judgment for the plaintiff in this matter.
