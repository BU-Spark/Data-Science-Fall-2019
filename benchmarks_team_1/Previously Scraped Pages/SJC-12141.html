    07/26/2016 #5 ORDER: Either side, or both sides, may file a new brief if they wish. In light of what has been said in the plaintiffs' motion and the defendants' opposition, a few caveats are in order. First, any new brief will be considered by this court in lieu of the brief that was filed in the Appeals Court. See Mass. R. A. P. 27.1 (f); see also 81 Spooner Rd., LLC vs. Zoning Bd. of Appeals of Brookline, 461 Mass. 692, 693 n.1 (2012); Beal Bank vs. Eurich, 448 Mass. 9, 10-12 (2006). A party may rely on the Appeals Court brief or file a new brief, but may not do both. Second, the role of this court on further appellate review is generally not to adjudicate the correctness or incorrectness of the Appeals Court decision; we do not affirm or reverse decisions of the Appeals Court on further appellate review, rather we decide the case anew as it was before the Appeals Court, focusing primarily on the correctness or incorrectness of the trial court ruling that is on appeal. While the parties may thus wish to discuss the Appeals Court's decision in their new briefs, and while it may be appropriate to do so to a limited extent, they should not be under the mistaken belief that our objective is to determine ultimately whether the Appeals Court was right or wrong, and should focus instead primarily on the trial court order. Finally, the parties should note that any arguments made in applications for further appellate review (or oppositions) are not before the court unless made in their briefs. The court considers the parties' briefs, not the applications that brought the case before the court, when it decides the case on the merits. It is therefore ORDERED that the briefs of the parties shall be filed on or before September 6, 2016. By the Court.
