    04/15/2009 #15 MEMORANDUM AND ORDER (Cordy, J.): Neil S. Nicoll, the defendant in Comm. v. Nicoll, 452 Mass. 816 (2008), has filed a motion for appellate attorneys' fees pursuant to MRCrimP 15(d), as appearing in 422 Mass. 1501 (1996). In that case, Nicoll was charged with operating a motor vehicle under the influence of alcohol. Comm. v. Nicoll, supra. Six jurors, but no alternates, were empanelled; after the trial began, one juror had to be excused. Id. at 816-817. The case was scheduled for retrial, and Nicoll moved to dismiss on the ground that the second trial would violate his right to be free from double jeopardy. Id. at 817. The judge agreed, reasoning that Nicoll could have waived his right to be tried by six jurors. Id. at 817, citing MRCimP 19(b), 378 Mass. 888 (1979). Therefore, there was no "manifest necessity" for the mistrial, and a second trial would violate Nicoll's right to be free from double jeopardy. Comm. v. Nicoll, supra. The Commonwealth appealed, and we transferred the case on our own motion. Id. We agreed that there was no "manifest necessity" for the judge's decision to declare a mistrial, and therefore affirmed the judgment of dismissal. Id. at 822. Nicoll is entitled to attorneys' fees under MRCrim 15 (d). That rule states, in relevant part: "If an appeal or application therefor is taken by the Commonwealth (from a decision granting a motion to dismiss), the appellate court, upon the written motion of the defendant supported by affidavit, shall determine and approve the payment to the defendant of his or her costs of appeal together with reasonable attorney's fees to be paid on the order of the trial court upon the entry of the rescript or the denial of the application." Nicoll has filed the appropriate motion under MRCrimP 15(d), and his attorneys (Barbara J. Sweeney and Charles K. Stephenson) have filed affidavits demonstrating that their costs and fees totaled $9,686.25. See Fabre v. Walton, 441 Mass. 9, 10-11 (2004)(establishing procedure for supporting amount of attorneys' fees sought). The Commonwealth has not filed any objection to this motion or to the amounts described in the affidavits. Id. Because the case falls under the statutory rule, and because the attorneys' fees are reasonable, we award costs and fees in the requested amount of $9,686.25. (Notice to counsel.)
