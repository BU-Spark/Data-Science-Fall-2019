    08/01/2016 #42 ORDER: The petitioner, Daniel D. Tavares, has been charged with various crimes, in, it appears, three different indictments, including possession with intent to distribute cocaine, in violation of G. L. c. 94C, § 32A (c), and larceny over $250, in violation of G. L. c. 266, § 30(1). The cases originated with complaints in the District Court and were subsequently transferred to the Superior Court after the indictments issued. Accordingly, bail was initially set in the District Court and subsequently transferred to the Superior Court. After the transfer to the Superior Court, Tavares filed a G. L. c. 211, § 3, petition in the county court claiming that he has been ordered to pay bail several times in the same case, and that the Superior Court improperly forfeited bail, or a portion of bail, in one of the cases. In his petition Tavares asked the court to, among other things, vacate the bail orders; return the forfeited bail; and vacate and dismiss the indictments. A single justice denied the petition, and Tavares appeals. Filing a petition pursuant to G. L. c. 211, § 3, is the proper means by which to seek relief from bail determinations in the Superior Court, see Commesso v. Commonwealth, 369 Mass. 368, 372 (1975), and Tavares's appeal from those determinations is therefore properly before the full court and may proceed. Although his appeal from the bail forfeiture is not, standing alone, similarly suited to review pursuant to G. L. c. 211, § 3, we will consider the claim here in connection with, and where it is related to, his claims regarding the bail determinations. To the extent, however, that Tavares has raised other issues while his case has been pending in this court, including claims of ineffective assistance of counsel, those issues were not before the single justice, are thus not properly before this court, and will not be considered. The parties should therefore address only the bail related issues in their respective briefs and should, in particular, address the application of this court's decision in Commonwealth v. The Stuyvesant Insurance Co., 366 Mass. 611 (1975), to the bail forfeiture circumstances presented here. The case will be considered at the court's November, 2016, sitting. In lieu of the regular briefing, either party may, if the party wishes, rely on the papers filed with the single justice. A party choosing to proceed in this manner must file the requisite number of copies of the single justice papers on which the party relies. Both parties should notify the Clerk whether they intend to file a brief or proceed on the single justice papers. By the court.
