"it is ORDERED that the matter shall be remanded to the Probate and Family Court for the purpose of conducting such an evidentiary hearing, and for an inquiry as to whether Cervone is competent enough to retain Sud as her attorney in the matter of the disposition of her home or an on ongoing basis. In connection with this hearing, the judge shall appoint independent counsel, who has had no previous involvement in this case, to represent Cervone. See Zaltman, 65 Mass. App. Ct. at 674 ("In connection with [the evidentiary hearing to determine whether the ward is competent enough to retain her own counsel], independent counsel must be appointed to represent [the incapacitated person]"). The hearing shall be conducted forthwith, and the judge shall make written findings describing the grounds for the decision reached. The written decision, and a transcript of the hearing, shall be returned to this court no later than thirty days after the conclusion of the hearing." (Lenk, J.)
