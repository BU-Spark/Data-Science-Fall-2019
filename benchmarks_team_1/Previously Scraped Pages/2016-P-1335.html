    10/17/2016 #11 ORDER: In this case, the Superior Court entered judgment for the plaintiff against certain of the defendants and dismissed the claims against the other defendants, thus resolving all claims, on September 2, 2015. On September 16, 2015, the Superior Court docketed the "[p]laintiff's [n]otice of intent to file [m]otion to [r]econsider[, a]lter [o]r amend [j]udgment on defendant's [c]ounsel". The plaintiff did not then file the Superior Court Rule 9A motion package, including the allowed reply to the opposition, until January 6, 2016. The motion was denied, and the Superior Court entered the denial on its docket on April 8, 2016. On Monday, May 9, 2016, the plaintiff file a notice of appeal. On Tuesday, May 24, 2016, the defendants against whom the judgment entered filed a notice of appeal. Under the circumstances of this case, rule 4(a) of the Massachusetts Rules of Appellate Procedure requires that a notice of appeal to have been filed within 30 days of the judgment unless a Rule 59 motion was served within ten days of the entry of the judgment. It does not appear, given the date of filing, and the title of the plaintiff's September 16, 2015 filing, that the Rule 59 motion was served on the defendants within ten days of the entry of judgment, i.e. service completed by September 14, 2015. Therefore, on or before October 31, 2016, the plaintiff is ordered to move for voluntary dismissal of the above-captioned appeal pursuant to Mass. R.App .P. 29 (b), or to show cause, in writing, why jurisdiction over this appeal is proper. The cross-appellant/defendants' notice of appeal was filed 15 days after the plaintiff filed its appeal. "If a timely notice of appeal is filed by a party, any other party may file a notice of appeal within fourteen days of the date on which the first notice of appeal was filed, or within the time otherwise prescribed by this rule whichever period last expires." Mass. R.App. P. 4(a). The time for filing an appeal from the judgment, as prescribed ty the rule, expired no later than May 9, 2016. Assuming arguendo that the plaintiff's notice of appeal was valid, the other parties, including the cross-appellant/defendants had until May 23, 2016 by which to file its notice of appeal. Therefore, on or before October 31, 2016, the cross-appellant/defendants are ordered to move for voluntary dismissal of the above-captioned cross-appeal pursuant to Mass. R.App .P. 29 (b), or to show cause, in writing, why jurisdiction over this cross-appeal is proper. Notice/attest
