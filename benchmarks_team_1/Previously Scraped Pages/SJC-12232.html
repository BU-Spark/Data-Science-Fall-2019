    03/03/2017 #6 ORDER: The petitioner, Jahmal Brangan, stands indicted for armed robbery while masked. Brangan was unable to post bail in the amount set at the time of his arraignment. His trial ended in a mistrial, see Commonwealth v. Brangan, 475 Mass. 143 (2016), and Brangan is being held while awaiting retrial.(n.1) After our decision in Brangan, supra, Brangan filed a petition seeking bail review, which a single justice of this court remanded to the Superior Court. A judge in the Superior Court set bail in a reduced amount, which Brangan asserts he is unable to pay. Brangan filed a renewed G. L. c. 211, § 3, petition in the county court seeking bail review. The same single justice denied the petition, and Brangan appeals. The case is before us pursuant to S.J.C. Rule 2:21, as amended, 434 Mass. 1301 (2001). Filing a petition pursuant to G. L. c. 211, § 3, is the proper means by which to seek relief from bail determinations in the Superior Court. See Commesso v. Commonwealth, 369 Mass. 368, 372 (1975). As Brangan has no other means of obtaining adequate appellate review of the bail determination, this appeal may now proceed, with briefing and oral argument. (n.1) It appears that Brangan has since moved to dismiss the indictment and that a single justice of this court denied his petition for relief from the denial of that motion. Brangan has separately appealed to the full court from that judgment. Notice sent.
