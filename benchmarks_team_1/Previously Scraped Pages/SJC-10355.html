    01/15/2009 #4 ANNOUNCEMENT: The Justices are soliciting amicus briefs. In this challenge to the validity of a warrant authorizing GPS monitoring of the defendant's vehicle, the issues presented, among others, are: whether GPS tracking constitutes a search or seizure within the meaning of the Fourth Amendment or article 12 of the Declaration of Rights; whether the defendant lacked a reasonable expectation of privacy in the movements of his van on a public way; whether the execution of the GPS warrant was valid under the provisions of G. L. c. 276, s. 3A; whether the defendant was deprived of the effective assistance of counsel, where counsel failed to raise issues relating to the GPS warrant in the motion to suppress. Argument is tentatively scheduled for May 2009. Amicus submissions are due on or before April 20, 2009.
