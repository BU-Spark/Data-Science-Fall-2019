    01/03/2012 #23 The Massachusetts Office of Public Collaboration, among others, has filed a brief as an amicus curiae in the above-captioned case, which is pending before the court and is scheduled to be heard at the court's January sitting. On November 21, 2011, the court issued an announcement entitled Strategic Planning Process to Launch Early Next Year, a copy of which is attached. The announcement describes the process by which the court, in conjunction with the Administrative Office of the Trial Court, will engage in long-term strategic planning for the court system. The courts will be assisted in that effort by the Massachusetts Office of Public Collaboration, which has contracted with the Division of Capital Asset Management to facilitate the process. Chief Justice Ireland is not presently aware of any reason why he must be recused in this case. No aspect of the work of the Massachusetts Office of Public Collaboration on the court's strategic planning process is at issue in the case, and the issue before the court is not something that will arise in the strategic planning process. The Chief Justice has satisfied himself that he has no preconceived views on the merits of the litigation and that he will be impartial in judging the appeal. Chief Justice Ireland requests that both sides indicate if they believe there is any reason why he should recuse himself in light of the involvement of the Massachusetts Office of Public Collaboration both in the strategic planning process and as an amicus curiae in this case. The clerk shall enter this statement, along with any responses received from the parties, on the docket for the appeal.
