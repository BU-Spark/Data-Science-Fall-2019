    12/27/2016 #30 ORDER: We have carefully reviewed the defendant's request for attorney's fees and costs incurred as a result of the Commonwealth's appeal from the dismissal of the indictments against him. In particular, he seeks fees and costs incurred in applying for direct appellate review; in preparing a brief and for oral argument in this court; in preparing a motion to provide grand jury minutes for interested amici; and in preparing the motion for fees and costs. Where the fees and costs result from the Commonwealth's appeal, the Commonwealth is required to pay reasonable fees and costs pursuant to Mass. R. Crim. P. 15 (d). We find no merit to the Commonwealth's arguments, raised in opposition, that it should be relieved of this obligation because the defendant "invited" the motion judge's "clear error" or, in the alternative, that the fee award should be paid entirely by the Trial Court rather than shared by the Commonwealth and the Trial Court as legislatively mandated. Although the defendant is entitled to fees and costs, we think that the amount that counsel is seeking -- $53,460 in fees and $1,130 in costs for a total of $54,590 -- is excessive in the circumstances. Although counsel prepared several different documents, including an application for direct appellate review, a brief, and a motion to provide redacted grand jury minutes to interested amici, there was significant overlap between some of those documents, particularly the application and the brief. Furthermore, counsel has represented the defendant throughout the proceedings -- in the trial court as well as in this court -- and was therefore intimately familiar with the facts and the law of the case before the appeal. Lead counsel also has indicated that the single legal issue in this case is one that she has previously presented in other cases, and she was therefore already versed on the issue, prior to her work in this case. The defendant's motion for fees will be allowed in the amount of $20,000, and costs are to be awarded in the amount of $600. This order may be enforced, if necessary, by the Superior Court. By the Court
