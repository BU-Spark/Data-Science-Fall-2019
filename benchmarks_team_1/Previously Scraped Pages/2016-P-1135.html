    05/10/2017 #13 ORDER: Before us is the Commonwealth's appeal from an interlocutory order of a District Court judge, excluding as hearsay certain testimony of Adams police officer Gregory Charron. The Commonwealth argues that Charron's proffered testimony is not hearsay because it is a statement of the defendant and an adoptive admission. See Mass. G. Evid. § 801(d)(2)(A),(B) (2017) (statement offered against an opposing party and "made by the party" or "is one the party manifested that it adopted or believed to be true" not hearsay). The defendant made his statement to Charron during the course of his arrest for assault and battery in a domestic dispute. The defendant opposed the Commonwealth's motion in limine in part on the ground that his statement was both in violation of his Miranda rights and involuntary. Were we to agree with the Commonwealth about the statement's admissibility as an adoptive admission, the defendant might still argue that his statement should be suppressed. To avoid piecemeal litigation and serial appeals, we remand the case to the District Court for an evidentiary hearing and written findings on the issues of Miranda and voluntariness. The District Court shall hold the evidentiary hearing and transmit its findings to this court within sixty days of this order. The panel shall retain jurisdiction of the appeal. (Agnes, Massing & Lemire, JJ.). [2] *Notice/Attest. Footnotes 1. Charron would testify that he told the defendant that the defendant's wife (the alleged victim) had told him that the defendant "grabbed her by the hair." The defendant responded, "[Y]ea[h] I did but that's all I did." 2. The panelists are listed in order of seniority.
